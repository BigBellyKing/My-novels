Chapter 241: No One Can Stop Me From Being the Azure Star! (Requesting Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

With Chen Xu's official Weibo account stepping forward, many Souls series players with masochistic tendencies, who had been looking forward to experiencing the long-lost dancing on the blade's edge and suffering in Monster Hunter: World, felt a pang of regret.

However, what more players cared about was the content revealed in Chen Xu's reply.

A pure cooperative hunting game, not a Souls-like game, and it features equipment collection and upgrade gameplay.

Many players were taken aback.

Because the few simple sentences on the official Weibo account revealed too much content.

In the Parallel World, co-op games were not a rare genre.

However, pure co-op games were relatively uncommon.

Most still adopted a setting where versus and co-op coexisted.

After all, in pure co-op, all content must revolve around map levels and BOSS design.

Content involving player versus player is simpler to create and can also better maintain a player's engagement time in the game.

So, pure co-op games were quite rare.

As for hunting games, although there were a few decent series, they were also very rare, usually releasing only one title every two or three years.

This was because it was difficult to establish a core gameplay loop.

Even some games originally centered on hunting either underwent reskinning or significant reforms.

More often, hunting was relegated to FPS games and ARPGs, serving as a system setting for bonus mini-games.

Making it a standalone genre was truly rare.

If there was any difference, it was that Chen Xu's Monster Hunter: World this time turned the hunting targets into dragons.

Similarly, countless attentive players were also animatedly discussing the fact that Monster Hunter: World was a hunting-themed game.
