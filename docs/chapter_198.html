Chapter 298: Entertainment-filled RPG Maps (Requesting Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

Just like when they first encountered Warcraft, many players were filled with novelty upon entering the RPG maps.

The same scene unfolded for other players.

After experiencing these Warcraft maps with different attributes, players quickly discovered their unique fun.

Each map had its own characteristics, but they shared one common feature.

Unlike Warcraft's inherent RTS gameplay, players essentially only needed to control one unit.

They didn't have to worry about anything else.

This greatly reduced the game's operations.

In particular, some players who couldn't fully appreciate the fun of RTS in Warcraft instead found enjoyment in these distinct RPG maps.

Especially when playing Warcraft before, they basically valued every unit highly. Faced with low-health units, they often wouldn't send them to their deaths directly, but would instead preserve them or restore their health with healing potions.

Because units were truly precious in Warcraft; what died wasn't common soldiers but money.

However, in RPG maps, the hero's existence is primarily emphasized, with one skill often wiping out a large group of common soldiers.

This gave players an indescribable sense of exhilaration.

Before this, Warcraft had given them the impression of a hardcore RTS.

But now, they felt the same sensation as playing a story campaign, only even more exhilarating.

After all, the story campaign itself was also responsible for familiarizing players with the basic concepts of Warcraft's RTS gameplay, so it couldn't be completely unrestrained.

But now?

These RPG maps had no such concerns at all.

The sense of exhilaration from cultivation and growth was instantly unleashed, getting players addicted to these maps.
