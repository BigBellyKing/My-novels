Chapter 241: No One Can Stop Me From Becoming the Azure Star! (Requesting Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

Following Chen Xu's official Weibo post, many masochistic players of the Souls series, who had been eagerly anticipating the long-lost dance on a knife's edge and suffering in Monster Hunter: World, felt a wave of disappointment.

However, what more players were concerned about was the content revealed in Chen Xu's reply.

A pure Hunting Co-op Game, not a Souls-like game, and it also features equipment collection and enhancement gameplay.

Many players were taken aback.

Because the few simple sentences on the official Weibo account revealed far too much.

In the Parallel World, Co-op games were no longer a rare genre.

However, purely Co-op games were still relatively uncommon.

Most still adopted a setting where competitive and co-op play coexisted.

After all, for purely co-op games, all content had to revolve around map levels and BOSS design.

Content involving player versus player combat, on the other hand, was simpler to create and could better maintain a player's engagement time in the game.

Thus, purely Co-op games were quite rare.

As for hunting games, while there were a few decent series, they were also quite rare, with releases typically every two or three years.

Because it was difficult to establish a core gameplay loop.

Even some games originally centered on hunting either underwent reskins or significant reforms.

More often, hunting was integrated into FPS and ARPGs as a system setting for bonus mini-games.

Making it a standalone genre was simply too rare.

If there was any difference to be noted, it was that in Monster Hunter: World, Chen Xu's latest development, the hunting targets were dragons.

Similarly, countless attentive players were discussing animatedly that Monster Hunter: World was a hunting-themed game.

Over a dozen types of usable weapons, each offering a different experience.

Clearly, there was no 'class division' in this game; all players had only one identity: hunter.

And what weapon to use was entirely up to the players themselves.

One weapon was equivalent to one class. If you got tired of one, you could just switch to another weapon.

Additionally, the game's environments, such as the Ancient Forest, Wildspire Waste, Coral Highlands, Rotten Vale, and Elder's Recess, were gradually unveiled to players.

They were truly breathtakingly beautiful.

Besides these, there was also related game lore, such as simple introductions and explanations of the Hunter's Guild and the Azure Star.

Combined with this environment concept art and the gameplay Chen Xu had previously mentioned, a rough outline had already formed in the minds of all players.

Players would take on the role of a hunter, embarking on adventurous hunts in a vivid and lifelike world.

The targets of these hunts would be powerful dragon-like creatures, and ultimately, through repeated hunts, players would become legendary marvels of the hunting world, guided by the still-legendary Azure Star, who would show the way for future hunters.

Simply perfect!

Using a variety of weapons to hunt different types of dragons and collecting corresponding materials to craft equipment.

Ultimately becoming the great Azure Star.
