Listening to the voice outside, Zhang Yi looked bewildered. Kratos then opened the door, and standing there was a man covered in tattoos with a full beard.

Compared to the Kratos who would ruthlessly slaughter his way through Olympus at the slightest disagreement, he seemed incredibly insane. But Kratos, having a child, had clearly changed completely. There were no massive bodies like those of the Greek gods and Titans. This mysterious person was even shorter than Kratos. The visceral strikes, combined with wide-swinging, powerful attacks and highly visually impactful performance effects, were stunning. Huge tree trunks were destroyed by their combat, and hard rocks crumbled under their blows. While they competed in strength on the mountainside, the entire mountain range was torn in half by their immense power, creating a bottomless canyon. From attacking with trees and pummeling the opponent against rocks, enhanced by activating Spartan Rage, this violent aesthetic, though different from the epic grand scenes before, was even more shocking to players.

In the end, Kratos still proved superior, snapping the mysterious person's neck and throwing him down the valley.

Zhang Yi was still immersed in the fierce battle. Although it wasn't against a massive monster, the impact and shock made him sigh, feeling that this combat style was what God of War should be. Besides that, what made Zhang Yi most emotional was that Kratos might truly be old. In ancient Greece, Kratos slaughtered countless Olympian gods, enduring countless earth-shattering battles. But he always slayed gods and Buddhas, showing no signs of fatigue. But now? At the end of the battle, Kratos, covered in blood, lay on the ground, breathing heavily. He only got up after resting for a while, staggering and dragging his axe as he returned. Anyone could see that Kratos was truly exhausted after the great battle.

Combined with how Atreus had previously been afraid to even harm an elk, this was clearly a strong psychological impact for him. Indeed, Atreus fell into confusion, and at this time, Kratos didn't yell but patiently guided him. Setting aside Kratos's identity, at this moment, Kratos was a kind old father.

On their way to the highest mountain range, Kratos and Atreus encountered some special stories.
