Chapter 19: Shock

As a game editor, even though Wu Tian hadn't been on the job for long.

Still, when it came to games, Wu Tian had played many.

From the current content of Undertale, Wu Tian could clearly perceive some of its characteristics.

First was the game's quality, which, of course, wasn't about the content but purely the external quality, such as graphics and operational experience.

Undertale, in his opinion, was only passable, not much different from a typical Independent Game.

But regarding content, Wu Tian could only say that Undertale, as it currently felt to him, wasn't great.

The puzzle content, though only for the tutorial level, was quite dull and uninteresting.

It was just pushing rocks and pressing levers.

As for the combat phase, the game offered some dialogue options, such as "Mercy."

Players didn't have to defeat the monsters.

Based on Wu Tian's gaming experience, this was nothing more than a honor value system.

High honor value meant a good ending preference, while low honor value meant a bad ending.

There were no special settings, and the game's plot itself felt like a generic dungeon crawling template.

After eliminating some small monsters blocking his way and seeing the post-combat prompts, Wu Tian continued his journey without overthinking it.

As a game editor, his approach was to first complete a playthrough to experience the entire process.

Although a relatively peaceful solution was possible, Wu Tian still chose the simplest method: combat.

After all, in an RPG, isn't fighting monsters to level up perfectly normal?

As he progressed, Toriel frequently appeared in the game, asking the protagonist what they liked to eat and what they were allergic to.

As he explored further, Wu Tian lightly sighed and shook his head.

The mother goat Toriel, who had previously saved the protagonist, stopped her from leaving, stating that if she insisted on going, she would have to prove her ability to protect herself.

Just like previous battles, combat against Toriel also offered the "Mercy" option.

Unlike ordinary monsters, Wu Tian quite liked Toriel.

So he didn't attack, but he soon found that the "Mercy" option was useless.

Or would it be a plot-kill scenario, where he'd empty Toriel's health bar to trigger a cutscene?
