Chapter 282: Stay Steady, We Can Win (Requesting Subscriptions and Monthly Passes)

With the launch of Warcraft, the game's popularity surged.

After all, Warcraft was excellent in every aspect. Whether it was the graphics, the story, or the gameplay, it gave players a sense of novelty and amazement.

Besides its surging popularity, sales also skyrocketed. Especially overseas, Warcraft, despite launching a week later than domestically, still caused quite a stir. After all, the production quality was simply too exquisite; neither domestic nor international RTS games had ever reached Warcraft's level of quality.

As for sales, Warcraft had already recouped its investment by the first week. Many designers in the gaming industry looked on in astonishment and envy.

Of course, envy was envy, and astonishment was astonishment. But genuinely, not many designers or game developers had the courage to undertake such a project. After all, Warcraft succeeded, but what if it had failed? High risk, high reward, but the risk was too immense; for many game developers, failure would mean eternal damnation.

During the period following Warcraft's launch, the RTS game market essentially heard only one voice. Both players and media gave the game exceptionally high ratings. Game media and player aggregate reviews consistently hovered around 9.5 points.

'RTS Ushers in a New Era of Warcraft!'

'What is the Charm of Warcraft, Recommended by Over Ninety Percent of Renowned RTS Players?'

'Buy a CGI Animated Film, Get a Game for Free: Warcraft's Stunning Story and CGI!'

'Redefining RTS Games, It Undeniably Succeeded!'

'A Simple Analysis of Arthas's Character Transformation and Jaina's Mental Journey.'

All sorts of news articles and topics were related to Warcraft. About its sales performance, its game quality, and its content. For a time, Warcraft's prominence was unmatched.

In contrast to Warcraft's glory, Hero Entertainment's Human Wars on the other side was looking rather bleak.

A week after Warcraft launched, Human Wars bit the bullet and released an update regarding its remaster. Originally, this should have been fairly good news. Because before this, Human Wars had announced a free remaster, providing players with a game overhaul and better visual presentation. Along with new version content updates, coupled with Human Wars itself being the number one RTS game domestically before Warcraft, this news had many players excited and expectant.

But that was before; now, it was completely different. Before, everything was just words; no one knew who was bluffing and who was truly impressive. But now, Warcraft's quality was out in the open. Comparatively, Human Wars' remastered visuals were a bit dismal.

Of course, it was indeed much better than the old version, with some detailed improvements. But the key was that during this period, players' standards had largely been raised by Warcraft. Not only was the CGI exquisitely produced, but Warcraft's in-game models themselves were also very refined. Faced with the Human Wars remaster, many players' first reaction was to compare it to Warcraft.

But that comparison led to big trouble.

Then, Warcraft's quality emerged, giving players an incredibly stunning impression, which subconsciously raised expectations for Human Wars' performance. But once it was truly revealed, those expectations immediately plummeted into an abyss.

Meanwhile, inside Hero Entertainment, Zhou Qi was utterly helpless.
