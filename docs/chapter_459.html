Song Ren was utterly speechless looking at the Animus that had sent him back out.

He could still connect, but he found that once connected, it only displayed the central city area of Florence.

If it had been before, Song Ren might have enthusiastically wandered around a bit.

But having just finished experiencing the game, he now had no such mood. It was simply too much!

He was having such a great time!

Why did it suddenly end? Intentionally stopping the trial content here, Mr. Chen was simply too much. Song Ren was speechless; this cliffhanger was absolutely brilliant!

Although he felt that Assassin's Creed's gameplay was quite free, for example, he could dart through the streets and alleys of Florence, parkour on rooftops, and swim in the water.

However, he could also clearly sense that Assassin's Creed, much like Uncharted, placed a lot of its focus on the game's storyline.

More importantly, the cliffhanger in this trial was placed with exquisite precision.

Here, the player, portraying Ezio, witnesses his father, younger brother, and older brother tragically betrayed by Uberto, the fat Magistrate.

At the same time, Ezio finally seemed to transition from a thug to the path of an Assassin.

He also had his Hidden Blade repaired by Da Vinci and learned how to conceal his Assassin's footsteps.

Everything was ready, just waiting to head to the target location to assassinate the traitor, when it suddenly cut off.

This was absolutely maddening. After all, at this point, all players were immersed in the game and had high expectations for the upcoming assassination and revenge sequence.

But? It was gone!
