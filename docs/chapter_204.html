At the center of the meeting room, Chen Xu explained some related conceptual settings.

The settings for attackers and defenders, and the three different gameplay modes: Bomb, Hostage, and Secure Area.

There was also a PVE Terrorist Hunt Mode, including tutorial arrangements for new players.

After all, Rainbow Six Siege itself is a very hardcore game. Some basic content and game philosophy still need to be clearly understood by players.

Of course, considering that the tutorial content might be slightly duller than the normal game, and taking into account players' psychological feedback, the simplest method for tutorials is reward-based onboarding.

Operator cosmetics and weapon skins will serve as the enticing goals for players.

As for the monetization model, compared to RTS games which are more difficult to set up payment systems for, FPS games are much easier to design in this regard.

In addition, concerning the Operators, players will need to unlock them using Renown after entering the game, just like in the previous life. However, for starter Operators, players can freely choose six from the ten available.

Of course, the unlock conditions for Operators are more lenient than in the previous life, avoiding situations where Grind Masters had to spend tens of thousands of Renown to unlock one early on. The initial 10 Operators can be unlocked for 1500 Renown each, while subsequent new Operators will cost 5000 Renown.

The main purpose of this is to help players better understand the game's settings and familiarize themselves with each Operator's skills and abilities. This prevents situations like your Fuze teammate 'thump-thump-thumping' a room, and you, as a teammate, rushing in without a second thought.

The core fun of the game, map mechanics, weapon system attachments, and so on.

Demolition... no, fortification and breaching walls and wooden panels to open up an attack route is a core gameplay mechanic of the game itself.

As for the maps, the focus was on various routes and planning destructible environments. Chen Xu would sketch out simple designs, and Ruan Ningxue's team would produce the art assets. Yang Xin was in charge of the game's background story.
