Chapter 203: Industry Discussions (Seeking Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

Initially, the META game concept in Undertale, developed by Chen Xu, opened up a new line of thinking for many Game Designers in the industry.

Following this, the independent game scene also saw an explosion of META game fever.

The second time was with the release of Overcooked and Fall Guys. Their cooperative and variety show-style gameplay similarly inspired many Game Designers.

However, subsequent titles like Dark Souls and It Takes Two, despite also providing inspiration to many designers, revealed different insights.

They highlighted the lack of excellent two-player cooperative games and that players' acceptance of hardcore games was higher than imagined.

But in terms of the games' actual gameplay and settings, they generally offered little practical reference for many Game Designers.

This was because the improvement came from overall quality, and many small studios and independent game designer teams simply couldn't bear the cost risks.

Now, the emergence of Don't Starve and its so-called sandbox game genre, has brightened the eyes of many Game Designers.

Though, as of now, all Game Designers have yet to fully grasp the core concept of Don't Starve or the concept of sandbox games.

For instance, what exactly is the success factor of Don't Starve? Is it the survival gameplay, the collection and building elements within the game, or other hidden reasons?

Nevertheless, it's undeniable that this type of game, Don't Starve, is completely worth trying.

Because it has a fundamental element: Don't Starve is not a big-budget production.

Judging by Don't Starve's art assets and its finished scale, it's clearly a standard independent team game.

And small-scale production means low trial-and-error costs.

Furthermore, from the overall external framework, many designers feel that replicating the success of Don't Starve is much easier than that of Dark Souls.

Because the theme of Don't Starve is very clear: it simulates a rich sandbox world and allows players to survive better within it.

The only question is how to achieve this in other sandbox games.

On a certain industry forum, many renowned independent Game Designers, as well as designers from second and third-tier companies, were discussing the pros, cons, and direction of Don't Starve.
