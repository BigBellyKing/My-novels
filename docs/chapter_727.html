The current version of Sacred Warrior, Chen Xu had designed according to its reworked version in his previous life. The first skill was Inner Fire, which reduced healing and disarmed, not Berserker's Blood. Of course, Sacred Warrior's armor and initial attack were slightly nerfed.

As for Shadow Fiend Nevermore, he adopted the version where his ultimate had a fear effect, and his Shadowrazes could stack a damage amplification debuff.

Upon entering the game, Song Ren didn't check the side lanes. Instead, he meticulously blocked his creeps.

However, his basic last-hitting skills were clearly a bit rusty. He quickly missed a creep, and when the creep waves finally met, they were perfectly positioned on the Dire's high ground.

But Song Ren didn't care, because his Sacred Warrior had learned Burning Spear as his second skill.

Manually casting it wouldn't draw creep aggro at all.

Seeing the enemy Nevermore, Song Ren didn't flinch at all and immediately started trading auto-attacks with him.

One Burning Spear after another pierced the enemy, causing a burning effect.

However, the enemy Nevermore wasn't panicked at all. He just traded auto-attacks back. But because he was near the Dire's tower and had trees nearby, Nevermore immediately consumed a Tango.

At the same time, he used the gaps between auto-attacks to distance himself from the melee creeps. Although he couldn't dodge the ranged creeps' projectiles, his positioning allowed Nevermore to avoid several melee creep attacks.

Next, because he had drawn creep aggro, the Dire creeps were constantly attacking the Radiant creeps. When the nearest creep was at low HP, Nevermore's hands suddenly lifted, and a fiery red shadow bloomed beneath Sacred Warrior's feet.

Shadow Fiend's first skill, a mid-range Shadowraze.

"This Shadow Fiend didn't learn Necromastery, he learned Shadowraze!?" Song Ren was utterly shocked.

Among ordinary players, most Nevermore players still primarily focused on the second skill, Necromastery.

This was because the skill increased attack damage upon successful last-hits, and a level one Shadowraze didn't feel like it did much damage. In most cases, having two levels of Shadowraze at level three was considered the kill threshold.

But now, things were a bit different. Sacred Warrior's Burning Spear had a characteristic: it was a thousand damage to the enemy, eight hundred self-damage. Although he was happily attacking, he was losing a good amount of his own health.

More importantly, he had initially planned to trade health, and then at level two, he would learn his third skill, and slowly recover.

But now it was different. Although Shadow Fiend's HP was still continuously dropping, he still had a little over a third of his health left, and only 5 stacks of Burning Spear debuff. Meanwhile, Song Ren's HP had already entered the kill threshold.

No sooner had this thought appeared than Song Ren saw the Shadow Fiend in front of him cast another close-range Shadowraze. His health was instantly depleted.

He subconsciously tried to control his hero to retreat, but the next second he regretted it. Shadow Fiend raised his hands again, unleashing the third Shadowraze.
