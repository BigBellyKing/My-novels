Chapter 276: First Experience (Requesting Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

Sitting in front of his computer, Zhang Yi was also eagerly awaiting the unlock of the Warcraft trial version experience.

"Ah, why did Nebula Games make an RTS game this time?" Wang Han, sitting next to Zhang Yi, was scrolling through information on her phone, chatting with Joey.

When it came to RTS games, neither Wang Han nor Joey particularly liked them.

They had played them, but couldn't get the hang of them.

Because they felt the controls were too complicated.

At most, they'd play against AI opponents.

"I feel like Warcraft might be different," Zhang Yi said.

He himself occasionally played RTS games, but wasn't very skilled; he was just at an average person's level.

He was the type to silently build up an army, then drag a box with his mouse and A-click them towards the enemy base.

A single decisive push: if his units all died, then the opponent won.

If his units didn't all die, then 'sorry, your base is gone'.

"Exactly, it's hard to say about Warcraft's multiplayer, but I feel the single-player mode should be quite good," Song Ren and Wang Yifan said in the voice channel.

"What kind of story can an RTS have? The single-player story in Human Wars almost put me to sleep," Wang Han said.

She had played some RTS games before, thinking if she couldn't win multiplayer and found simple AI opponents boring, she might as well try the single-player story.

The result was truly disappointing, to sum it up in one sentence.

It was always: the enemy is attacking, so we need to do this and that.

We defeated the enemy and won, but then new enemies attacked again.

It really almost put her to sleep.

"You can't compare other developers to Chen Xu. I still have a lot of faith in him, even if he always pulls some tricks," Zhang Yi said, full of confidence in Warcraft.

Especially that CG animation, it truly shocked Zhang Yi.

He had never seen a game company invest so much in an RTS game before.

Moreover, the short one-minute CG video for Warcraft at the time had also made Zhang Yi very curious.

Because he felt that Warcraft was different from typical RTS games, which often just turned their so-called single-player stories into tutorial levels.

He felt that Warcraft would genuinely have a complete single-player story.

Recalling Chen Xu's previous mention of subverting the 'RTS genre' and the '700 million development cost'.

Zhang Yi also had a bold guess: would Warcraft put a lot of effort into its story?

Like most players, he thought that Chen Xu, venturing into the RTS field for the first time, might struggle with balancing issues in multiplayer mode.

But a single-player story was different.

This tested level design, and considering Chen Xu's level design in Dark Souls and It Takes Two, was there any room for doubt?

So he believed there was a high probability that Chen Xu was focusing on the single-player story for Warcraft.

He still had considerable confidence in games produced by Nebula Games.

He even secretly used his private stash of money to buy a Collector's Edition of Warcraft!

Of course, he definitely couldn't let her know, or else he might end up doing all the laundry from now on.

After all, buying the Collector's Edition of Warcraft wasn't the key point; the key point was that he had a private stash of money.

"Alright, alright, you play first and I'll watch. If it's fun, I'll try it myself later; the game isn't going anywhere anyway," Wang Han said to Zhang Yi.

"It's time! Old Zhang, I'm muting my mic!" Song Ren's excited voice came from the voice channel.

"O.J.B.K!" Zhang Yi replied and immediately clicked on the Warcraft icon.

Entering the game, the Nebula Games LOGO flashed by.

The first thing was a CG animation.

It was the same CG promotional animation Chen Xu had previously released on the official Weibo account.

Zhang Yi had already watched it more than once, but this time he still chose not to skip it.

The CG content was relatively easy to understand.

It meant that Humans and Orcs were not friends, but opposing factions, but a sudden evil enemy descended from the sky, forcing Orcs and Humans to unite.

Soon the CG animation ended, and the game's UI interface appeared.

The background was a scene resembling an iceberg, presumably an in-game location.

Aside from the exit game and settings options.

Zhang Yi observed that there were three different choices in the game.

Story Campaign, LAN local multiplayer, and online multiplayer.

However, online multiplayer and LAN were currently grayed out, clearly not supported in the trial version.

But this made Zhang Yi excited, because judging from the content of this trial version, Chen Xu clearly wanted players to experience the single-player story, which perfectly matched his guess.

"Let me see how this single-player story is!" With an excited heart, Zhang Yi eagerly clicked the Story Campaign button.

The Story Campaign was no different from other games; difficulty could be chosen before entering.

Easy, Normal, and Hard.

Considering this, Zhang Yi thought carefully for a moment and chose the easiest difficulty.

"You're really embarrassing yourself," Wang Han next to him couldn't help but say, speechless.

If it's the easiest, then it's the easiest. What's the point of your mouse hovering back and forth between 'Hard' and 'Normal' for three or four seconds and then choosing neither?

"Cough, cough... This is Mr. Chen's game after all. Think about Dark Souls and Monster Hunter: World. I'm just being proactive," Zhang Yi explained with an awkward cough.

After selecting the difficulty, what appeared on Zhang Yi's screen was 'Chapter One'. Clearly, this single-player story was a linear game, requiring players to clear it chapter by chapter.

After clicking on Chapter One, Zhang Yi was stunned.

Because there were simply too many levels in this first chapter!

He counted briefly, there were a full twelve sub-chapters!

However, only the first one was lit up, displaying the level name: 'Thrall's Vision - Seeking the Prophet' (The original prologue and Chapter One were merged).

The rest were still grayed out, their level names unclear.

"I just wonder how long each level will be," Zhang Yi grew even more expectant.

He directly clicked on the first prologue level.

The screen gradually faded to black, and narration began.

'The sands of time have run out, Son of Durotan.'

'The horns of war echo in the wind, the lingering shadows of old poison the land, and blazing war once again scorches the world.'

'Leading thousands of troops to dye the battlefield with blood, mortals blindly rush towards their doom, unaware that the Burning Shadow is about to devour all things.'

'You must gather the Horde and lead them to their destiny!'

On the battlefield, human armies and orc armies were clashing. Then, just like the previous opening cutscene, countless fireballs burning with green flames rained down from the sky.

The next moment, the camera switched to a high mound, where an Orc who had just awoken from a nightmare looked disoriented in a barracks.

This segment of CG was essentially a completion of the content from the previous promotional CG, but it also raised many questions and curiosities for Zhang Yi.

Humans and Orcs battling, what exactly was that fiery figure descending from the sky?

What was the Burning Shadow?

And who was that mysterious mage at the end?

Controlling the Orc named Thrall who had just awakened from a nightmare, Zhang Yi's heart was filled with curiosity.

Curiosity aside, he quickly refocused his attention on the game.

The character named Thrall advanced under the Prophet's guidance.

This was a basic game operation tutorial.

But what surprised him most was the overall quality of the game.

Zooming in the camera, he could even clearly see the exquisite modeling of the characters in the game.

Including the textures of the water on the ground and the grass, all were quite exquisite.

Was this really an RTS game?

The quality was simply too high!

He hadn't even played the game's content yet.

But just the modeling and graphics of Warcraft, combined with the earlier CG, had already left Zhang Yi in awe.

It could only be said that the current performance of Warcraft completely exceeded his expectations.

Compared to the CG animation, there was indeed a noticeable gap.

But compared to other RTS games, even without considering the content, the presentation alone was far superior!

(End of this chapter)
