As for the map, it was NI, also known as the Northrend Islands.

And each player only had one expansion base next to them.

Satellite, playing as an Orc player in the game, slowly let out a breath of relief upon seeing this map.

On this map, he dared to say that if Chen Xu still used his previous mining strategy, he would surely be doomed!

"It's the same single burrow fast tech, with Blademaster as the first hero, just like before. But note the very tricky position of Satellite's burrow; he chose a backward position close to the gold mine."

"Eh! What's happening? The Lumber Mill is up, six Peasants immediately transformed into Militia and are heading towards Satellite's base. He summoned a neutral hero, the Firelord. What is going on?!"

Huang Donglai on the commentary desk stated that he couldn't understand it.

Equally bewildered was Satellite in the game.

He had a default opening. After the fourth Peon and the first Grunt and Blademaster came out, his scouting was complete at the center of the map. He saw a Human Footman near the tavern, which suggested a Human default opening with Barracks first, then a neutral hero. However, what he didn't expect was that the opponent directly chose a Firelord, which he didn't quite understand.

Wouldn't Archmage be better?

However, when he saw the second Footman behind it, and the swarm of Militia rushing over, Satellite became even more confused.

As a professional player, he had studied all the game's data. In that instant, he understood why the opponent didn't pick Archmage as the first hero, but Firelord instead.

Because at level one, Firelord is stronger than Archmage, and the Infernal summoned by Firelord also has a splitting characteristic; if not dealt with, it splits and doubles its combat power.

Also, starting directly with a neutral hero saves time and allows for an extra Human Footman.

But the key thing was, you want to take him out in one go with 1 hero, 2 Footmen, and 6 Militia?

You must be dreaming!

Militia aren't permanent.

Thinking this, Satellite immediately controlled his Blademaster to activate Wind Walk, ran over, and struck the Firelord with a slash.

In the early game, especially at level one, there was almost nothing that could contend with a Blademaster.

Satellite's idea was to draw the Militia's attention and buy some time.

But the next moment, Satellite was completely bewildered, because Chen Xu moved the Firelord to the side, but its direction was still towards his base. And with just that slight maneuver, it took two more slashes from his Blademaster, leaving it with only three-quarters of its health.

'Yeah, Firelord how can beat Blademaster in the early game? Even with Militia, there are only twenty seconds left. Once time's up, won't they all be easy targets for Blademaster?'

Chen Xu in the game, meanwhile, was smiling broadly.

Especially when he arrived at the Orc base and saw that very far-back burrow, he smiled even more widely.

Located next to the Orc Barracks, 800 yards away from the burrow (burrow attack range is 700 yards), 6 Peasants immediately began building Guard Towers.

This was also a characteristic of Humans: it cost lumber and gold, and multiple Peasants could build structures simultaneously.

Once a Guard Tower was completed, he upgraded it directly to an Arcane Tower without hesitation.

Before long, the second Guard Tower was also completed.

And the Lumber Mill at his base was also ready, so he immediately upgraded it to an Arrow Tower.
