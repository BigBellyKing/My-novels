Inside his rented apartment, Chen Xu looked at the sales statistics in the backend of his official platform, feeling a bit surprised.

Because the overall growth in the data had truly exceeded his expectations.

On the first day, sales were 162 copies, a number neither high nor low.

On the second day, sales directly quadrupled, reaching a full 610 copies.

The main reason for this was his promotion.

With a budget of twenty thousand yuan, a normal advertisement certainly couldn't have been run.

On one hand, the money wasn't enough; on the other hand, the game had a slight peculiarity.

So Chen Xu's gaze immediately turned overseas, to a forum frequently visited by gentlemen.

After all, for Chen Xu, what he wanted was exposure.

As for the method, it was a relatively primitive approach of elite astroturfing posts.

Chen Xu hadn't considered large-scale astroturfing bombardment.

Because that wouldn't achieve a good effect, and might even backfire.

So the method Chen Xu adopted was to buy accounts and go for an elite approach.

Buying some older accounts on the forums, then publishing review posts.

After posting, astroturfers from other platforms would then bump the posts' content.

Of course, the most crucial point here was that the game's quality had to be up to standard.

Otherwise, this promotion method would not be as effective as large-scale astroturfing.

The most crucial point for this model's success was to convert players who learned about the game into allies, making them spontaneously recommend it to their friends and fellow gentlemen.

And as a professional Game Planner and an honest and simple player in his previous life, Chen Xu naturally understood a principle very well.

"Hmm, at this rate, I'll be able to pay back the money owed to the payment app next week."

"But just like in my previous life, the commission from these third-party platform channels is really a bit hard to bear."

Thinking of this, Chen Xu couldn't help but click his tongue.

The official game platform takes a 30% commission, mainly for daily platform maintenance, as well as game engine upgrades and iterations, and the development of the latest technology.

As for other third-party game platforms, they are far more exploitative.

Especially when it comes to mobile channels, they typically start at 50%.

Some even go as far as a 70-30 split, where the developer gets 30% and the platform takes 70%.

This is comparable to the Goose Factory and major mobile app store channels in his previous life.

However, there are benefits, of course: these third-party platforms have a considerable user base, and the official platform offers fewer recommendations and its algorithms are not scientific.

It's just that they only get the broth, while the platforms take all the meat.

But for Chen Xu currently, these platforms were only briefly considered and held no interest at all.

The main reason was that Mirror (Game) was a bit special; even if it were on those platforms, they wouldn't grant any recommendation slots.

Of course, this applies to domestic platforms, whereas overseas platforms have less strict restrictions.

But with the official platform available, there was no need to use third-party channels.

Because those domestic platform operators actually have no significant influence overseas.

Their main focus is domestically.

Just as some forums are filled with various curses and controversies, in forums frequently visited by gentlemen, it's different.
