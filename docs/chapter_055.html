There were many, many more players like Song Ren. Initially, these players learned about Outlast because of To the Moon. But more often, they found out about the game through forums in various gaming communities.

Just like Song Ren, these players, after seeing comments from other players, were filled with a sense of curiosity and a competitive spirit. How scary could a horror game possibly be? Is it really that big a deal? Aren't these just advertising trolls?

With such thoughts, these players all planned to play the game themselves and then post screenshots of their completed game to the forums to show off. After all, many players, especially when it comes to courage, aside from those who truly know their limits, quite a few have an enigmatic confidence in themselves.

However, once they entered the game, they quickly understood what kind of game it truly was. Not to mention the later content, just the initial parts had already scared away a large number of players.

Some players, full of ambition, entered the game only to be deterred at the very entrance of Mount Massive Asylum. Others were deterred during the first door jump scare or when they encountered the Fat Guy.

Of course, even more players were deterred in the basement, facing the pitch-black environment where they couldn't see their own hand, and being chased by a madman wielding a spiked plank.

Naturally, a portion of players gritted their teeth and persevered. However, after they repaired the generator and the Priest gave them an injection, entering the prison section, they realized that what came before was merely an appetizer!

Of course, within the game, they were deterred at different stages. But after playing, one action they all took was common. That was, their posts appeared on game forums, either recommending or warning other players.

It was like a matryoshka doll, one nested inside another. After all, if I got scared, then I might as well trick as many as I can. Others should feel the same way I do!

The barrage of information left some later players completely bewildered, unable to comprehend what kind of horror game Outlast truly was. Because some, to make their claims more convincing, deliberately took screenshots of less horrifying scenes.

However, others spoke in great detail, stating that Outlast was extremely terrifying. More and more accounts also attracted subsequent players who learned about Outlast, who then eagerly plunged into the game one after another.

Just like a relay race, where the person ahead reaches the finish line and then passes the baton to the next athlete. But the spread among Outlast's player base was even wider.

Because the player community was continuously expanding, this meant that the dissemination of information was also constantly growing. Initially, for relevant directions and topic content, Chen Xu's side still needed to subtly guide and appropriately create memes for the players.

But as more and more players peed their pants in Mount Massive Asylum, Nebula Games' capital no longer needed to provide guidance. The players' spontaneous discussions were already enough to create hot topics, fermenting little by little like a prairie fire and continuing to expand its influence.
