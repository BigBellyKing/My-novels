Chapter 266: A Truly Legendary Game (Sixth Update, Requesting Subscriptions and Monthly Passes)

Monster Hunter: World continued to sell well, and player enthusiasm remained quite high.

In fact, there was still a fair amount of work planned for Monster Hunter: World in the future.

Although essentially a single-player game, it was one with strong vitality.

Moreover, in Chen Xu's plans, several subsequent versions would be launched.

This included classic monsters from the previous life's Monster Hunter series, such as Tigrex, Zinogre, Brachydios, Ukanlos, and Bulldrome...

However, these were all contents to be released later. For the current version of Monster Hunter: World, the content was already quite substantial.

Similar to the DLC for Dark Souls, Chen Xu also planned to establish a dedicated project team to slowly develop these subsequent contents.

As for what remained, it was the previously established strategy.

Further consolidating the advantages of the Nebula Games Platform. While Minecraft was a long-term game, for many players, it leaned more towards casual leisure.

Monster Hunter: World, on the other hand, currently had a very active player base.

However, as the content deepened, the rate of player churn would inevitably be faster than expected.

Therefore, for Chen Xu, he also needed a game that could later take on the role of Monster Hunter: World in boosting user stickiness for the Nebula Games Platform.

For this new title, Chen Xu chose a game that could be called legendary.

An RTS competitive game: Warcraft.

Initially, Chen Xu had his sights set on MOBA games.

Whether it was DOTA from his previous life or LOL.

For Chen Xu now, both users and capital were sufficient.

And the development difficulty wasn't that high.

But after careful consideration and market research, Chen Xu decided to temporarily postpone the MOBA strategy.

There were two main reasons.

Firstly, MOBA games had not yet emerged in this world, and even RTS was still in a developmental stage. Rashly releasing a MOBA game would likely make it difficult for most players to understand its gameplay mode.

In the Parallel World, RTS games were relatively prevalent.

Some settings that Chen Xu considered important had largely been perfected.

For example, the fog of war in games.

In his previous life, the concept of fog of war actually emerged due to game engine capabilities.

Its embryonic form first appeared in Dune, the perfected concept was in Patton Versus Rommel, but it was truly popularized by Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness.

In fact, many classic game designs originally stemmed from compromises due to insufficient technical capabilities.

In the Parallel World, the appearance of the fog of war wasn't due to technical limitations, but designers still proposed and created corresponding concepts.

There were even many disputes, for example, designers sued each other for several years over who first proposed the fog of war design, with no definitive conclusion even now.

Secondly, what Chen Xu found more concerning was that the esports industry in the Parallel World was much more vibrant compared to his previous life.

This wasn't surprising, as the gaming industry in the Parallel World was larger and held a more esteemed status than in his previous life.

In such a situation, status, reputation, and attention were all abundant.

Naturally, a decent competitive game giving rise to professional leagues was a normal occurrence.

For instance, on the Channel Alliance side, the game with the highest concurrent player count was an RTS game called Human Wars.

This was also why Chen Xu chose Warcraft.

Judging by the various maneuvers from the Channel Alliance, NetDragon, and TengHua since the release of Monster Hunter: World, they clearly wouldn't sit idly by.

Chen Xu also had no interest in waiting around.

When he announced a new game, it was highly probable that the Channel Alliance, NetDragon, and TengHua would immediately follow up to target it.

In that case, he might as well take the initiative.

When a potential enemy might threaten you, eliminate them before they can pose a danger.

How great would that be!

Therefore, after comprehensive consideration, Chen Xu decided to develop Warcraft first.

After making the decision, Chen Xu gazed at the computer monitor and pondered.

To plan the general framework.

Speaking of outstanding RTS games from his previous life.

There were many.

The early RTS pioneers like Age of Empires and Red Alert, and the competitive benchmark StarCraft. But if one were to name the RTS game with the most profound influence.

Then nine out of ten players who understood what an RTS game was would say Warcraft.

Because it was simply too classic, especially the third generation.

Setting aside the game's story content, purely in terms of game mechanics, Warcraft could truly be considered the magnum opus of the series, having genuinely forged its own style.

Population, and each unit having different tech trees and skills, forming combinations. Especially the introduction of hero units, which could be said to have revolutionized the entire RTS genre.

To some extent, it was precisely because of the introduction of heroes that MOBA games later rose to prominence.

Particularly with its final version, The Frozen Throne, Warcraft truly ascended to godhood.

Perhaps many players weren't clear on Warcraft's story, but they certainly knew the two main characters in The Frozen Throne storyline.

Illidan and Arthas.

Even when World of Warcraft was later released, many Warcraft players were drawn in because of these two characters.

Although these two characters didn't appear until Wrath of the Lich King and The Burning Crusade, and were merely content for players to grind in dungeons.

However, for Chen Xu, he currently only planned to incorporate the storylines of Reign of Chaos and The Frozen Throne from Warcraft III into the main game content.

As for the earlier stories like Orcs & Humans, Tides of Darkness, and Beyond the Dark Portal, Chen Xu planned to gauge player feedback.

If enthusiasm was high, then he would release corresponding single-player campaigns as DLC.

If the response was lukewarm, then he would follow the old tradition of Blizzard Entertainment from his previous life and release animated shorts.

Besides, there was another crucial point: the Map Editor.

"One game, ten thousand ways to play" was the praise players gave to Minecraft before its emergence.

But before that, this title belonged to Warcraft.

It's important to note that the reason Warcraft had such immense vitality in his previous life, besides its excellent quality, was largely due to the Map Editor feature.

Including DOTA and LOL, both had intricate connections to it.

After roughly outlining a few directions, Chen Xu stretched and walked out of his office.

Although it was an RTS game, Warcraft was definitely not small in scope. And for Chen Xu, he wasn't planning to treat it as a small-scale RTS.

If he was going to do it, he would bring this legendary game from his previous life to the pinnacle that Nebula Games could achieve.

A quick note: I won't be writing about LOL and DOTA after War3 for now, even though it's currently the season for World Championships and TI.

(End of this chapter)
