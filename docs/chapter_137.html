Chapter 236: Is This Really a Hunting Game? (Sixth Update, Please Subscribe and Vote for Monthly Pass)

Before the computer, Chen Xu rested his chin.

Since the decision had already been made to create Monster Hunter: World, he still needed to consider how exactly to go about it.

From a gameplay perspective, Monster Hunter: World was a very special game.

In other multiplayer games, there would always be some PVE elements.

But Monster Hunter: World, on the other hand, was a purely cooperative hunting game.

In Dark Souls, players could even invade other players' worlds.

Moreover, for many Dark Souls masters later on, the fun was essentially in PVP.

However, Monster Hunter: World contained no PVP content.

The game emphasized player teamwork and cooperative hunting of dragon-like creatures.

As for Demon Mode Dual Blades slashing teammates, charging Lances piercing friends, Light Bowguns blowing up a teammate's crotch, Heavy Bowguns penetrating a teammate's defense, or Great Hammer's three-charge home run, all the above scenarios were exceptions, absolutely not PVP content of the game.

From the game itself, whether it was the game's background content, Monster Hunter: World could be said to be a game with a very unique style.

Firstly, the core selling point of this game was its action system and a relatively realistic hunting experience.

These all served to create the overall hunting experience of the game.

In typical games, battles between players and bosses usually lasted a few minutes, at most ten minutes.

However, in Monster Hunter: World.

When facing powerful bosses, for most ordinary players.

A fierce battle lasting fifteen to twenty minutes was considered quite normal.

Of course, speedrunning masters, or Grind Masters who had already ground out a set of powerful equipment to bully smaller dragons, did not count.

Paired with the AI's performance, Chen Xu believed it could showcase the immersion of hunting even better than Monster Hunter: World in his previous life.

Next was the game's class system.

Or rather, its weapon system.

The game had no distinctions like warriors or mages for classes.

Instead, it had different weapon classifications.

Just like how different weapons in Dark Souls had different attack methods.

In Monster Hunter: World, there were a variety of distinctly characteristic weapons for players to choose from.

At the same time, each weapon had different combo skills.

Much like a fighting game, players needed to execute specific input commands to successfully unleash weapon moves.

Of course, in actual operation, it wouldn't be like fighting games where players needed to memorize long move lists.

Furthermore, it wasn't impossible to play if one couldn't execute weapon combos.

It just meant one couldn't pull off very flashy maneuvers.

Additionally, regarding difficulty, compared to Dark Souls.

Monster Hunter: World was much simpler.

Because the game would feature a gradually progressive difficulty pace.

To put it bluntly, Dark Souls makes you get abused first and then slowly familiarizes you with the game.

Whereas Monster Hunter lets you familiarize yourself with the game before getting abused, which is considerably more humane.

Furthermore, Monster Hunter: World itself was a very replayable game.

Why was this the case?

When you've farmed for hundreds of hours and still haven't gotten a single Attack Jewel, you'll naturally understand why this game has such high replayability.

Because the game inherently possessed a 'grinding' attribute, but if it were just grinding, it wouldn't be enough to keep players engaged for such extended periods.

Ultimately, even when facing the same monster, each hunt was unique.

There would still be a different experience.

Changes in the battle environment, other bosses potentially intruding, unstable teammate factors during cooperative play, and the monster's own attack patterns.

All these factors ensured that every hunt could unfold differently, preventing it from being monotonous and dull.

The most crucial point, however, was that in Monster Hunter: World, there were actually quite a few clever strategies that allowed players, even if their skills weren't top-notch, to successfully complete the game as long as they understood its characteristics.

Of course, it wasn't completely brainless either.

After two continuous days, having largely completed the design Concept Art drafts for Monster Hunter: World, Chen Xu called Qin Yi, Ruan Ningxue, and others to the conference room for a meeting.

Everyone arrived early at the conference room, each excitedly discussing in low voices.

"I wonder what kind of hunting game Mr. Chen mentioned earlier it's going to be!"

"Yeah, I feel like the setting should lean towards ancient times, with players primarily using weapons like spears and bows for hunting in nature."

"I'm more curious about how AI performance will manifest in hunting."

"Feeling a strange sense of anticipation!"

It was clear that everyone in the conference room had learned about the new game's hunting theme in the past two days, and they all looked full of anticipation.

Just then, Chen Xu pushed the door open and entered, and everyone quieted down.

Sitting at the head of the table, Chen Xu looked at everyone and said: "I believe you all already know some details about the new game, so I won't waste any more words."

"The main purpose of today's meeting is the development of the new game. Please look at the big screen."

As Chen Xu's words fell, the game title Monster Hunter: World appeared on the projector.

"First, let's briefly talk about the game's theme and direction."

"Regarding the theme, this is a hunting game, but the combat system will be quite unique, differing from other action games currently on the market."

"At the same time, within the game, we will create a relatively vast ecological world to showcase a characteristic of AI intelligence."

"Finally, the monsters players will hunt are primarily dragon-like creatures... well, these."

Chen Xu slid his finger across the touchpad of his laptop.

On the projector screen in the conference room appeared a colossal monster, with yellowish-green carapace, powerful wings, and limbs.

Its ferocious claws and sharp teeth made it look incredibly terrifying.

Beside it was an introduction to this monster: [Queen of the Land ‚Äî Rathian]

Looking at the image on the screen, everyone was stunned.

The sense of shock brought by this wyvern was simply too powerful.

"Furthermore, in the game, players will not have class divisions; instead, there will be different weapons. Simply put, weapons will replace what are known as classes."

As Chen Xu continued his introduction, the images on the conference room's projector screen also changed.

Great Sword, Long Bow, Dual Blades, Long Sword... a series of weapons appeared on the screen.

Of course, they all shared a common characteristic: all weapons were melee weapons.

Looking at the depiction in the Concept Art, although the Hunter wearing leather armor held a large weapon, compared to the Rathian from before, it still felt like a younger brother.

Besides this, there were some unfinished sketches. For example, one sketch depicted a colossal Ancient Dragon with outstretched wings covered in thorns, and two horns on its head that were even more chilling.

"Mr. Chen, is the new game... a Souls-like games?" Qin Yi swallowed, asking the confusion in his heart.

Colossal dragon-like monsters, tiny Hunters, and melee weapons.

Is this really a hunting game?

It's not going to be monsters hunting Hunters, is it?

Don't ask if there's more, just assume there is.
