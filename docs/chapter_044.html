Regarding the choice for the next game, Chen Xu had actually been deliberating earlier.

Firstly, it wouldn't be a small project like before; at least this time, it would move towards 3D games.

But at the same time, the scale couldn't be too large.

Although game development in the Parallel World was much simpler compared to his previous life,

This didn't mean that no technical accumulation was needed at all.

It was like building with blocks; everyone used the same parts, but what could be built depended on individual skill.

The same applied to games, especially FPS and ACT games.

For example, action games prioritize a sense of impact.

And how to create a sense of impact?

Sound effects, Controller Feedback, Special Effects, UI Interface Vibration, and various Physical Collision Detection. Additionally, a more crucial element was freeze-frame impact.

The same principle applied to FPS games.

So for his next project, Chen Xu wasn't planning on making an overly complex game.

‚ÄúThinking this way, a horror game doesn't seem impossible. It can incorporate some Action Elements, and for horror games, these Action Elements aren't the primary core,‚Äù Chen Xu stroked his chin, pondering.

Although he had an idea, Chen Xu didn't act immediately. Instead, he first did a rough online survey of the current state of horror games in the Parallel World.

Simply put, it wasn't much different from his previous life.

Whether in the Film and Television Industry or the gaming industry, horror games generally lacked Large-Budget Productions.

They were mostly the type that aimed for high returns with small investments.

However, compared to his previous life, the Market Share of pure horror games was considerably larger.

Some even saw Single Titles with nearly ten million sales.

Of course, these were older titles, usually sold at a cabbage-level price during holidays, and had been launched for many years.

Furthermore, there wasn't a single horror game specifically designed for VR platforms.

After all, VR games could be incredibly expensive in terms of Development Costs.

At the same time, horror games were inherently a Niche Category.

Unless someone had a special fondness for Horror Culture, no one would develop horror games for the VR platform.

After all, the risks were simply too great.

As for horror games on the PC platform, they were divided into two types: one was a Low-Cost approach.

For example, the model of Undertale and To the Moon.

Another type was made as a Medium-to-Small Scale game.

After roughly understanding the market and relevant developments of horror games in the Parallel World,

Chen Xu quickly made a decision.

To proceed with the Medium-to-Small Scale model, Nebula Games currently had some capital from the revenue generated by Mirror (Game), Undertale, and To the Moon.

Moreover, although the horror game market was niche, that was in comparison to Traditional Game Genres like FPS and RPG. If done well and it garnered buzz, horror games could still sell big.

Next, the question was which specific game to make.

For horror games, Chen Xu had quite a few directions to choose from.

However, after careful consideration, Chen Xu decided to choose Outlast as his next game.

Mainly, it was quite suitable.

In terms of Game Length, Outlast had a playtime of about 5 hours. Of course, speedrunning through the game would take less than an hour.

Secondly, compared to horror games like Resident Evil, Silent Hill, and The Evil Within, Outlast had a rather clever aspect.

That was, in this game, players controlled a Reporter who could only flee and not fight back.

For Chen Xu, Outlast was actually a game to integrate the team and allow him to more deeply test the Game Engine.

As for action and shooting aspects, they could be reflected in the game's monsters, while the Protagonist controlled by the player would perform basic actions such as running and climbing.

One could say that in all aspects, Outlast was a game that largely met Chen Xu's Psychological Expectations.

The only concern was the Censorship aspect.

After all, unlike Eastern Horror Games such as Silent Hill and Fatal Frame, Outlast was purely Western Horror, meaning the game was quite bloody.

Although the Parallel World's Review System was very comprehensive, whether it could pass the review still depended on the Game Department's decision.
