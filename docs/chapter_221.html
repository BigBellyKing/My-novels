Chapter 321: Ten Years Making Games, Nine Years Making CG (Requesting Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

The development progress of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare was exceptionally smooth. Although this was the team's second large-scale VR game, they had prior experience with Rainbow Six Siege, and NetDragon had provided significant technical assistance as part of their strategic cooperation. Consequently, they encountered virtually no major difficulties. The remaining task primarily involved art assets. Currently, Nebula Games had only developed Rainbow Six Siege, so their accumulated art assets were not extensive. Moreover, in terms of game experience, the two games were poles apart. All action systems, including the shooting modes, were completely incompatible. After all, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare aimed to deliver a fiery, swift, and thrilling fast-paced experience to players. For the vast majority of art assets, excluding core designs, everything else could be entrusted to outsourcing companies. In game development, outsourcing content, especially art assets, was a common practice, as it saved the team a considerable amount of time.

While Call of Duty: Modern Warfare was being quietly developed, NetDragon was also making significant moves. First, the PC version of Rainbow Six Siege was announced, along with plans to host related esports events. Chen Xu didn't intervene much in this aspect, as NetDragon had considerable experience organizing competitions and owned many competitive games. Furthermore, Nebula Games was largely responsible for content operation in the future. Simultaneously, information about Atrai's Storm Action was continuously relayed to China through the Channel Alliance. After all, this was a popular IP and Zeus's escort title. It was evident that Atrai had a meticulous plan. Alongside the announcement of the Storm Action game, the film's producer also revealed news about a Storm Action sequel. Clearly, this was a strategic strong alliance aimed at generating hype.

The Channel Alliance in China also held high hopes for this game. After all, the Nebula Games Platform's market share was continuously expanding. More and more third-party game developers were choosing to launch exclusively on the Nebula Games Platform. Although the Channel Alliance had progressively lowered their revenue split ratio, it was to no avail. Coupled with titles like Warcraft, Minecraft, and Rainbow Six Siege continually solidifying Nebula Games' player base, it could be said that they were slowly eroding the competition. Therefore, the Channel Alliance also placed high hopes on the blockbuster Storm Action. Besides offline exhibitions and press conferences, more and more news about Storm Action was revealed. Firstly, the actors playing the male and female leads would participate in the game's motion capture to ensure the authenticity of the characters. Subsequently, related game content and information were released, showcasing scenes from the movie and easter eggs. This greatly excited many fan players.

After all, several years had passed, and many players who had seen Storm Action still felt nostalgic for it. Now, with news of a new movie, game adaptation, and those familiar scenes, it immediately felt right to them. 'The fidelity is so high! And the graphics feel even better than in the movie!' 'Indeed, but I wonder what timeline and plot stage it's set in.' 'What I'm more interested in is the gameplay!' 'No need to doubt that, Atrai definitely has the strength!' 'I wonder if they can recreate the grand scenes from the original work's movie, especially that moment when the protagonist escapes the building, and the entire building is blown up. That was simply too spectacular.' 'I think there should be even grander scenes, right?'

Players expressed great anticipation upon seeing the content news released for Storm Action. After all, for an adapted IP game, the first impression for players was invariably about the graphics and the game's fidelity to the original work. Atrai was clearly fully aware of this; otherwise, there would have been no need to spend so much money acquiring the copyright. All information released to the public revolved around this point, instantly giving these fan players a shot in the arm. While the gameplay might still be unclear, the graphics and fidelity were already maximized. This could be considered a very conventional IP warm-up.

However, just as Atrai and the domestic Channel Alliance were continuously conducting their promotional warm-up for Storm Action, Chen Xu's official Weibo account also updated with a long-awaited post. The accompanying image showed a wasteland. Heavy machine guns, shoulder-fired rocket launchers... various weapons were displayed. There was also a group photo of some staff members. The accompanying text was just a simple sentence: 'Call of Duty: Modern Warfare, a game that defines what a modern warfare theme is, will launch on the Cosmic VR Platform!'

With the release of this official Weibo account post, all the players instantly erupted! Because this official Weibo account post had made it clear! Mr. Chen had been silent all this time, and he was making a new game again! And it was a modern warfare theme game. Looking at the accompanying images, Mr. Chen's scale this time was simply unprecedented! Heavy machine guns, shoulder-fired rocket launchers, and so on ‚Äì could it be that the game would feature all these real combat weapons? All the players immediately became restless.

'Alas, I'm uncultured; 'What the heck' suffices for all occasions!' 'As expected, Mr. Chen, the Quick Shooter, wouldn't endure solitude. He's about to do something big!' 'The surprise came too quickly, it caught me off guard!' 'So, is this going head-to-head with Atrai?' 'A game that defines modern warfare? Mr. Chen's tone is so arrogant! But I like it!' 'Looking at this posture, is it almost completed development!?'

Countless players were incredibly excited and thrilled. Although they knew nothing about how the game would look, only its theme and name had been announced so far. However, over the past few years, Nebula Games had accumulated enough popularity and reputation to fuel their anticipation. While countless players cheered online, Nebula Games was discussing with Qin Yi how to better showcase the action and gaming pod's force feedback system in Call of Duty: Modern Warfare, when Yang Chen from the Game Department called.

'Mr. Chen? How's the development progress of your Call of Duty: Modern Warfare? You absolutely must ensure the game quality, and don't worry about the tight schedule. Zeus's progress overseas is similar to ours, perhaps even slower, so Storm Action, which is escorting for Zeus, isn't developing as fast as imagined.' Minister Yang was rather nervous. Although Nebula Games wasn't the only game developer creating escort titles for the Cosmic VR Platform, giving players a strong first impression was quite important. Therefore, Minister Yang was concerned that Chen Xu might want to rush development, making a first move before Atrai to grab hype, thereby compromising the game content.

Hearing Minister Yang's words, Chen Xu smiled knowingly: 'Don't worry, Minister Yang, there's no way I'd joke around with Nebula Games' reputation. The development progress for Call of Duty: Modern Warfare is very smooth, and its quality is absolutely guaranteed. As for racing against others? We haven't even considered it.'

After exchanging a few words with Minister Yang and chatting for a while longer, Chen Xu put down the phone and smiled. Naturally, Chen Xu wouldn't rush development at the expense of game content. After all, the old saying applies again: no one remembers a garbage game that was released on time. Whether it's released on time or postponed, as long as it delivers satisfactory quality to players, everything will be worry-free. Otherwise, regardless of whether you release on time, postpone, or even early launch through reverse postponement, players will tear you apart so badly your own mother wouldn't recognize you. As for now? All he could say was, 'It's being made, it's being made.'

For Call of Duty: Modern Warfare, although the game progress was only about one-sixth completed overall. While there was still quite a long time until completion, the progress on the CGI front was extremely fast. After all, in Call of Duty: Modern Warfare, to ensure sufficiently outstanding visual effects, Chen Xu chose to mask most loading transitions with CGI. Combined with the various Operator CGI from Rainbow Six Siege and Warcraft's CGI, Chen Xu could only say that Nebula Games was absolutely professional in this area!

'Ten Years Making Games, Nine Years Making CG.' This was the online evaluation of Nebula Games previously, and Chen Xu stated it definitely had to be implemented thoroughly. Otherwise, how disappointed would players be? After all, he, Chen Xu, was a game designer who never lied and never disappointed players. (End of Chapter)
