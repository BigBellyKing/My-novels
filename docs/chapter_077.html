Chapter 176: Dark Souls Prequel, Launched! (Requesting Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

The launch date for Dark Souls: Age of the First Flame was drawing nearer.

Related advertisements had slowly begun to roll out across major websites.

Concurrently, pre-release promotion had also started in the Overseas Target Software segment.

The promotional efforts overseas were more substantial compared to the domestic market.

This was because, during July, most game developers had not yet prepared to release any new works.

On one hand, news had only just been announced at previous exhibitions, leaving insufficient time for development. On the other hand, Chen Xu had already announced early that Dark Souls: Age of the First Flame would be launched in early July.

As a sequel to an IP game, and specifically a sequel to the game that won Game of the Year for Best Role-Playing Game last year, with its launch date clearly stated, there were essentially no game companies stubborn enough to deliberately challenge it.

Similarly, for players, this period of waiting was utterly agonizing.

The promotional video from the previous exhibition had been rewatched countless times by players.

Combining various elements from the previous title, many Souls scholars had even created three-hour-long lore analysis videos based solely on that three-minute promotional clip.

Of course, besides these incredibly hardcore Souls scholars, many ordinary players were also quite afraid that Chen Xu's official Weibo account might suddenly post an announcement, stating that to provide players with an even better experience, they had decided to delay the release.

After all, game delays are quite common in the gaming industry, so these players were genuinely worried that Nebula Games might also pull such a move, even though Nebula Games had never done so before.

But that was also because, to date, Nebula Games hadn't released any large-scale games; if one had to name them, it would only be Dark Souls and It Takes Two.

However, as June began its countdown, more Dark Souls advertisements went live, simultaneously announcing the price of Dark Souls: Age of the First Flame. Especially with the corresponding pre-order interface appearing in the Nebula Games Platform store, many players felt a sense of reassurance.

This time, Dark Souls: Age of the First Flame was cheaper than its predecessor, priced at 198 Yuan compared to 268 Yuan. Additionally, as before, purchasing through the Nebula Games Platform granted a 20% discount.

As for overseas, the price also saw a reduction to $39.9 USD.

Due to time differences and relevant regional policies, the game would be launched simultaneously worldwide, with only a few regions experiencing a delay of about two days.

Time quickly arrived at the day nearing the game's unlock.

Numerous veteran Firelinkers had already pre-ordered and pre-downloaded the game, eagerly awaiting the moment to enter the game.

In community forums and various player chat groups, many waiting Dark Souls players chatted with each other, full of anticipation for the upcoming Firelinking journey.

However, these could only be opened later. Now that he had the Master Key, could it be used to open all sorts of shortcuts?

Of course, it could also have other uses, such as good items hidden in a room that required a specific key, and his Master Key could open it directly, or perhaps it was related to rescuing an NPC trapped in a room, like the thief in the previous game.

In any case, he felt that the Master Key should be a relatively good and useful item.

After creating his character, Song Ren clicked to enter the game with anticipation.

Ready to officially begin his Firelinking journey.
