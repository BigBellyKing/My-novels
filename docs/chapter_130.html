Chapter 229: Episode One: Major Historical Events in MC (Seventh Update, Please Subscribe and Give Monthly Passes)

Online, it wasn't just the designers in the gaming industry who felt shocked by the sales of Minecraft.

Players were equally surprised.

Before they knew it, tens of millions of people worldwide were playing Minecraft?

However, this was quickly replaced by a sense of pride.

How great is it that a game they loved had so many players!

Of course, this feeling only lasted for a moment.

After all, for players, it's just a game, so what if many people play it? A brief moment of excitement and pride is enough; what else would you want from them?

Compared to how many copies the game sold, players were obviously more concerned with the content within the game.

Initially, the videos with particularly high like rates online were mostly awe-inspiring structures built by Master Builders.

While initially shocking, with exclamations of 'others' worlds' constantly heard, players eventually got used to being amazed after seeing so many.

Anyway, they would just watch these, then perhaps download one from the Workshop to experience the fun for themselves.

But soon, the works of some Redstone Masters left many players bewildered.

All sorts of Redstone music, and Redstone mechanisms.

Some masters even used sticky pistons and other mechanism components to directly build a moving mech.

Many players were instantly stunned.

At least the previous constructions were somewhat understandable, but these Redstone contraptions were utterly incomprehensible!

But the point was precisely that they couldn't understand them; if they could, they wouldn't seem so incredible anymore!

And so, on this day, as many players were preparing to browse the official Weibo or check video websites for new content.

An official Weibo post quickly gained traction with frantic reposts, and simultaneously, a video was directly pushed to the top of the click rankings on major video websites.

The video's title was also very simple: 'Episode One: Major Historical Events in MC'.

Many players paused for a moment upon seeing this video, but as soon as they realized the publisher was Nebula Games' official Weibo account, they instantly became excited.

Clicking on the video, the progress bar showed it was nearly an hour long.

The visuals weren't particularly special; it was essentially raw in-game footage from Minecraft, unrendered at that.

But what truly captured players' interest was the content revealed within the game.
