Meanwhile, in the lounge, while Ruan Ningxue and the others were still making bold hypotheses and pondering carefully.

In his office, Chen Xu was refining the Concept Drafts for the upcoming game.

Monster Hunter: World, the project name for the game to be developed next, was displayed on the computer screen.

In fact, before this, thanks to the latest AI technology, Chen Xu had originally intended to develop other types of games.

For example, an open-world type game.

Compared to sandbox games, open-world games are arguably an equally great game genre.

But they are completely opposite to sandbox games.

Both share a common core: allowing players to feel free.

However, they also have completely different points of conflict.

Namely, sandbox games are about creating a world, while open-world games involve the designer creating a world for players to become a part of.

This is why many players often confuse these three different genres: sandbox, box garden, and open world.

And to present a vibrant open world to players, NPCs are an indispensable part.

With the latest AI technology, if NPCs in the game can behave more logically, it would undoubtedly lead to an explosive increase in player experience.

Because a lively world is the standard for an open world. Of course, exploration and puzzle-solving like in Breath of the Wild falls into another major direction.

However, after testing, given the current technology and player hardware standards, it was still insufficient to achieve the effect Chen Xu desired.

But for a small number of individual or multiple creatures, showcasing AI performance is entirely feasible.

Therefore, after some tests, Chen Xu decided to choose Monster Hunter, as it seemed quite fitting with the current feedback.

As for why Monster Hunter: World was chosen, it's mainly because it's quite special.

Monster Hunter: World is definitely a monumental installment in the Monster Hunter series.

One could even say that its release directly saved the Monster Hunter series, which was on the verge of death.

In his previous life, Monster Hunter had always been released on handheld consoles, but the series' sales continuously declined. Sales peaked at 5 million with P3, but by XX, they hadn't even reached 2 million.

So, the series finally decided to make a revolutionary breakthrough: move away from the weak performance of handheld consoles and release console and PC versions.

Ultimately, Monster Hunter: World captivated all players.

Because it was with this installment that players truly realized how magnificent the Ancient Dragons they hunted truly were.

And how splendid the world inhabited by Ancient Dragons truly was.

At the same time, the new Monster Hunter also received specific optimizations, such as the removal of loading zones. Players no longer had to ding and strike an awkward pose when drinking potions.

While preserving the authentic core fun, many negative experiences were optimized, making the overall game combat much smoother.

Of course, reduced difficulty was also a factor, as previous series had difficulties like Super Special and G-Rank 140.

In comparison, the Arch-Tempered Elder Dragon setting in Monster Hunter: World was much gentler.

After all, in Super Special and G-Rank 140 difficulty modes, our cute little hunter could be brushed by a dragon's foot, tail, or wing and might have to return to camp to regroup. How could a normal player withstand that?

Better catering to beginner experiences, coupled with a seamless open world, a complete and vibrant ecological chain, and thrilling hunting experiences.

These factors made Monster Hunter: World the best-selling installment in the series.

First-month sales exceeded 7.5 million.

In just one month, it broke the series' previous highest sales record.
