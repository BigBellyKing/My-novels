The concept drafts for Overcooked were explained, and the promotional direction was set. The entire team began preparing for the game's development.

While others were deeply understanding the concept drafts and refining the art, plot, and related designs, Chen Xu was not idle either.

In his office, Chen Xu used the Memory Capsule to organize and record some excellent level designs.

The development difficulty of Overcooked primarily stemmed from its level design and numerical design.

If it were too simple, the game would lose much of its charm.

Just like the Souls series games, difficulty isn't the sole core of a Souls series game, but it is definitely a major characteristic.

If a Souls series game turned into a Musou game, many of its original designs would no longer be brilliant but rather cumbersome.

Although Overcooked is a common small-scale game, the same principle applies: if the difficulty curve isn't managed well, the game's essence will be lost.

For example, chopping vegetables, washing dishes, preparing ingredients, and cooking, including the possibility of the kitchen catching fire and needing a fire extinguisher.

These are gameplay elements, but more important is the arrangement of numerical values.

If the time and score values are too high, it appears to be making things difficult for players.

But if the values are too lenient, then all these settings in the game won't seem very important.

Therefore, Chen Xu needed to properly control the difficulty.

As a casual game, it needed to allow players to pass levels smoothly.

However, achieving a three-star perfect rating would require a certain level of cooperation between two players.

Finally, there was the online system. Overcooked was also Nebula Games' first online game.

The server aspect definitely needed to be handled well.

It should be known that Overcooked was a game whose selling point was its multiplayer connectivity.

‚ÄúThe overall art style of the game leans towards warm tones. Even the zombies in the background should give players a Q-style cute feeling. This is an all-ages game, a casual simulation game. ‚ÄúUnderstood.‚Äù Ruan Ningxue nodded.

Because she felt her task was like an assembly line!

The style, and even the simple sketches and requirements for characters, were so detailed they couldn't be more so.

Handing it directly to an outsourcing company would immediately yield assets.

Wasn't the position she applied for 'Art Designer'? Why did it feel like she only needed to check the quality of outsourced assets, without doing any design herself?

A similar situation also occurred with Qin Yi and others.

However, with their previous experience on Outlast, no one was particularly surprised this time.

They only had one feeling: ease.

Although overtime was inevitable, the crucial point was that useless design work rarely occurred.

Areas that needed to be redone were mostly due to issues with details, not major directional problems.

It was simply too easy!
