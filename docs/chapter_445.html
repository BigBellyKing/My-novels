Players who enjoyed puzzle-solving genres gave it high praise. But praise aside, Atari and Zeus soon noticed a problem: the game's sales were off.

On the first day, sales were very impressive, thanks to the hype from the event and promotions. But as time went on, sales increased like an old woman crossing the road‚Äîlittle by little. This left Zeus and Atari a bit baffled.

To be fair, Element World's sales were actually quite high. But the crucial point was that Uncharted was overshadowing it, and Zeus had high expectations for Element World. So, why weren't sales climbing higher?

The reason for the stagnant sales was actually simple: the game's puzzles were too intricate and too difficult. But being 'good' wasn't enough; they didn't enjoy playing it, and found little fun in it. Even Souls-like games offered some enjoyment to ordinary players. For example, finding a veteran player to drop some items, or carefully relying on equipment to power through the first playthrough, or following a guide. And as action games, even with a guide, combat in Souls-like games still relied on the player's own skill. But puzzle games were different; they felt like being a puppet on strings, so even with a guide, the experience of a puzzle game was far inferior to that of Souls-like games.

This led to Element World's sales being far lower than Zeus had hoped. Sometimes the game industry is just that unreasonable: good player and media reviews? That doesn't necessarily mean a game will be a bestseller.

Atari's industry reputation wasn't particularly good before, but Element World gave them their first taste of widespread praise. Antorini felt the same; the high praise for Element World was a validation for him.

The only one feeling less than cheerful was Zeus. What's the use of a high reputation! It wanted sales, and to drive growth for the Zeus platform through those sales. Although there were undoubtedly puzzle game enthusiasts who bought Zeus specifically for Element World, just look at Uncharted next door. At this moment, Zeus thought about its investment in Element World and immediately felt a sharp pain in its chest.
