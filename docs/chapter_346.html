Call of Duty: Modern Warfare's popularity continued to soar.

Especially after most players experienced the story and then entered multiplayer versus, it became unstoppable.

The number of online players in multiplayer mode grew at an alarming rate.

Its fast-paced style, completely different from previous FPS games, instantly attracted a large number of FPS players.

Moreover, compared to the previous Rainbow Six Siege, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare was clearly much easier to pick up.

Just grab a gun and go; there's no economic system, so any weapon you want can be used directly after unlocking, complemented by a rich attachment system.

This allowed players to fully experience a different kind of fast-paced fun.

Coupled with the killstreak reward system, the game was even more enjoyable.

Summoning a UAV drone after a killstreak allowed players to scout enemy positions, just like having wallhacks.

After multiple killstreaks, players could even call in a bomber to bombard a specific area.

Compared to other traditional FPS games, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare's pace was incredibly fast and even more thrilling.

It also fully demonstrated the effectiveness of modern weaponry.

This directly elevated Modern Warfare's popularity to a new level.

At the same time, it left the gaming industry utterly dumbfounded.
