In its previous life, Final Fantasy VII was actually a tragic story, including Zack in Crisis Core.

However, in the later Final Fantasy VII Remake, the story was altered with the addition of a concept called Whispers.

These Whispers could be understood as fate, their existence meant to correct history to its intended path.

But the appearance of Sephiroth and Aerith created a singularity in fate.

Without such a setting, the original Final Fantasy VII in its previous life would have merely been a remake based on the original content. But with the inclusion of the unique Whispers concept, it undeniably represented the game's ambition and an interaction with the players.

Therefore, regarding this, Chen Xu made a decision to first let players experience the original story.

Because the Whispers concept actually requires players to understand the original story first to have a stronger sense of immersion.

And also a stronger urge to change regrets.

Although, from an artistic standpoint, tragedy is certainly more capable of moving people and making a deeper impression,

from a player's perspective, who doesn't want a truly happy ending?

So Chen Xu planned to divide the game into two versions. In the second version, the original fate would be rewritten by the players.

At the same time, in the base content of the first version, Chen Xu also enriched it, leaning towards the representation of the previous life's Final Fantasy Remake, emphasizing the roles of many supporting characters in the game.

For example, Biggs, Jessie, and Wedge in the original game were basically treated as nameless NPCs, but in the Remake, their roles were greatly expanded, especially Jessie, who even flirted with Cloud to the point of making him a little flustered.

Of course, why Biggs and Wedge were known to be alive, but only Jessie's fate was unknown, clearly demonstrated the bad intentions of the game developers in its previous life.

If popular, they live; if unpopular, they die.

Therefore, in the stories of these different characters, Chen Xu also planned a dual A/B storyline mode.

In version A, players would face various oppressive situations in the early stages of the game, including experiencing the encounters of characters like Vincent and Zack in the DLC.

And the introduction of Whispers in version B would bring new hope to the players.

A modern city, where all machinery was powered by Mako.

Looking at the yellow flower on the ground, Aerith carefully picked it up, then looked at the flower basket in her hand, lost in thought.

Slowly, Aerith pressed the flower to her chest, then slowly gazed up at the sky.
