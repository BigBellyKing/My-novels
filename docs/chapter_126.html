Chapter 126: Spreading the Hype

As players began to view Dark Souls more objectively, on the major Dark Souls game community forums, the players' discussions were no longer as aggressive as before. At the same time, the game's ratings also began to gradually rise.

Although there were still many players who bought and refunded the game, to Chen Xu, this was a very normal phenomenon. Because even though Dark Souls possessed a unique elemental charm, naturally, not all players enjoyed this kind of high-difficulty, hardcore challenge. For some players, Dark Souls was clearly not a game they would enjoy. However, for players who persevered, after defeating Gundyr the Ash and passing through the High Wall of Lothric, the unique aroma of this bottle of fine wine, the Souls-like game, fully diffused, captivating them deeply.

Secondly, in terms of spreading its influence, Dark Souls replicated the success of Outlast, and was even more popular. The main reason was that previously, no other game had been as unique as Dark Souls. Many players who had experienced Dark Souls, carrying their 'goodwill', enthusiastically recommended and promoted this game to their friends.

‚ÄúMr. Chen, about the follow-up arrangements?‚Äù From the Marketing and Operations Department, Zhang Yida was a little uncertain. Although he had considerable confidence in the quality of Dark Souls, Chen Xu also knew that a game's success wasn't solely dependent on its inherent quality; corresponding marketing and promotion were also extremely helpful for a game. Simply put, the game's inherent quality mostly guaranteed the lower bound of sales, but to achieve higher sales and influence, besides the game's quality, promotion and the creation of buzz were also extremely important. In his previous life, there was no shortage of such examples, the most well-known being Titanfall 2. In terms of quality, its reputation was phenomenal; apart from the single-player campaign being too short, it had no other flaws and surpassed its predecessor in every aspect, yet it ultimately flopped disastrously. Its core issue was its overly strong competitors: Battlefield 1, COD, and Overwatch, all powerful FPS rivals of the same genre, and their main focus was on multiplayer online modes. Coupled with the fact that it didn't secure any promotional resources, its fate was naturally a dead end. Therefore, regarding the mixed reviews from players online after Dark Souls' release, even though data showed no major issues, Chen Xu still confirmed some subsequent preparations with Zhang Yida, including guiding the game's trending topics. However, before they even made a move, the spontaneous efforts of players online had already steered Dark Souls in a positive direction.

After Zhang Yida left the office, Chen Xu sat in front of his computer and opened Dark Souls. He had also noticed the various online mentions from players tagging him on the official Weibo in his spare time. However, he had no plans for live streaming. But he should still capitalize on his own popularity when appropriate. No live stream, but recording some edited videos related to Dark Souls was perfectly fine.
