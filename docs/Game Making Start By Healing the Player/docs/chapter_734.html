Zhang Yi looked at Kratos's actions, his face full of surprise.

He had completely underestimated Kratos's willingness to do such a thing.

Protagonists who were outright villains, Nebula Games hadn't actually shied away from them before. However, they would always cloak their actions in a layer of justification.

For example, in The Last of Us, Joel could actually be described as a typical villain. If one were to view Joel from a moral and legal standpoint, there would be no way to associate him with being a good person. It was just that Joel's past misdeeds weren't shown in the game, and his later wrongdoings had Ellie as a medium, making it easier for players to empathize with Joel's perspective and thus not perceive his actions as excessively evil.

But the Kratos currently before him was much more direct; saving the captain seemed like a simple task, yet he didn't do it, instead leaving the captain to his own devices. Coupled with the previous violent visual impact, it immediately made Zhang Yi feel the brutality within this game.
