Meanwhile, in the lounge, while Ruan Ningxue and the others were still making bold hypotheses and careful conjectures.

In the office, Chen Xu was refining the concept drafts for the upcoming game.

‚ÄúMonster Hunter: World,‚Äù the project name for the game to be developed next, was displayed on the computer screen.

In reality, before this, thanks to the latest AI technology, Chen Xu had originally planned to develop other types of games.

For example, an open-world game.

Compared to sandbox games, open-world games can certainly be described as an equally great game genre.

Yet, they are fundamentally opposite to sandbox games.

Both share a common core: to make players feel free.

However, they also have completely different points of conflict.

That is, sandbox games are about creating a world, while open-world games are about designers creating a world and letting players become a part of it.

This is why many players often confuse the three different genres: sandbox, box garden game, and open-world.

And to present players with a vivid and lifelike open world, NPCs are an indispensable element.

With the latest AI technology, if NPCs in the game could behave more logically, it would undoubtedly lead to an explosion in player immersion.

Because a vibrant world is the standard for open-world games; of course, exploration and puzzle-solving like in Breath of the Wild fall into another major direction.

However, after testing, given current technology and player hardware standards, it was still insufficient to achieve the effect Chen Xu desired.

Yet, for a small number of individual or multiple creatures, demonstrating AI behavior was entirely feasible.

Therefore, after conducting some tests, Chen Xu decided to choose Monster Hunter, as it seemed quite compatible with current feedback.

As for why he chose Monster Hunter: World, it was mainly because it was quite special.

Monster Hunter: World was absolutely a landmark title in the Monster Hunter series.

One could even say that its appearance directly saved the Monster Hunter series, which was on the verge of dying out.

In his previous life, Monster Hunter had always been released on handheld console platforms, but the sales of the entire series had been continuously declining, reaching a peak of 5 million with P3, but failing to reach even 2 million by XX.

So, the series finally decided to make a revolutionary breakthrough: bidding farewell to the weak performance of handheld consoles and launching console and PC versions instead.

Ultimately, Monster Hunter: World captivated all players.

Because it was with this title that players truly felt how magnificent the Elder Dragons they once hunted actually were.

And how splendid the world where Elder Dragons resided actually was.

At the same time, some targeted optimizations were made for the new Monster Hunter, such as removing loading zones, and finally, there was no longer a need to ding and strike an awkward pose when drinking potions.

While preserving the original core fun, many negative experiences were optimized, making the overall game combat much smoother.

Of course, reduced difficulty was also a factor, as previous titles in the series still featured difficulties like Super Special and G-Rank 140.

In comparison, the Arch-Tempered monster mechanic in Monster Hunter: World was far too gentle.

After all, in Super Special and G-Rank 140 difficulty modes, our cute little Hunter might have to return to camp to regroup if merely grazed by a dragon's foot, tail, or wing. How could a normal player endure that?

Taking more care of the beginner's experience, coupled with a seamless open world, a complete and vibrant ecological chain, and thrilling hunting experiences.

Made Monster Hunter: World the best-selling title in the series.

First-month sales exceeded 7.5 million.

In just one month, it broke the series' previous highest sales record.
