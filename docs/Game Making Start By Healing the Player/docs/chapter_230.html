Chapter 229: First Episode: Major Historical Events of Minecraft (Seventh Update, Requesting Subscriptions, Monthly Passes)

It wasn't just gaming industry designers who were shocked by the sales of Minecraft online.

Players were equally surprised. Unbeknownst to them, tens of millions worldwide were now playing Minecraft?

However, this was quickly replaced by a sense of pride. How wonderful it was that so many people were playing their favorite game!

Of course, this feeling was fleeting. After all, for players, more players are just more players; a moment of excitement and pride is enough. What else could you want from them?

Compared to how many copies the game sold, players were clearly more concerned with the actual content of the game.

Initially, the videos with the highest like rates online were mostly of incredible structures built by Master Builders.

While initially awe-inspiring, with exclamations of 'Others' World,' players eventually got used to the feeling of being overwhelmed. Anyway, they'd just watch these creations, then head to the Workshop to download one and experience the thrill for themselves.

But soon, the creations of some Redstone Masters left many players utterly baffled. All sorts of Redstone music, and Redstone mechanisms. Some masters even used sticky pistons and other mechanism components to build a fully functional mech. Instantly, many players were stunned. At least they could understand the previous builds, but this Redstone stuff was completely incomprehensible!
