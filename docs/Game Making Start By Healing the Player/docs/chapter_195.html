Chapter 194: Don't Starve (7th update, requesting subscriptions and monthly passes)

As the screen displayed the image, everyone's attention was drawn to the Don't Starve Concept Drafts before them.

In front of the Concept Drafts, Chen Xu had already completed some simple art illustrations for the game.

In the wilderness, a dishevelled figure sat in front of a campfire, while distant spiders, wild boars, and other monsters slowly approached.

It wasn't colored, but the game's style was immediately apparent.

‚ÄúIs this a survival type of game?‚Äù

‚ÄúThis art style is so eerie; the rough lines give it a European and American cartoon style?‚Äù

‚ÄúIt's similar to It Takes Two and Overcooked (Chinese title). Is it a cooperative type of game?‚Äù

Looking at the Don't Starve game title and concept art, everyone present was quietly guessing what type of game it actually was.

‚ÄúJust like the game's name, Don't Starve, what players need to do in the game is not let themselves die. Survival is the sole core of the entire game.‚Äù

‚ÄúCollect stone, twigs, craft hunting tools, climb the tech tree, establish your own camp, then explore the unknown world to survive better.‚Äù

‚ÄúAlso, in the game, players might suffer a mental breakdown and go crazy from facing various eerie monsters, freeze to death from being too cold, starve to death from being too hungry, or die from attacks by wild monsters.‚Äù

Chen Xu explained the core idea of Don't Starve to everyone.

‚ÄúDoes anyone have any more questions?‚Äù Chen Xu asked.

As he finished speaking, Qin Yi stroked his chin while looking at the big screen on the Concept Drafts: ‚ÄúSo this is a survival game? But Mr. Chen, if it's too difficult, won't players stop playing?‚Äù

Ruan Ningxue beside him also nodded: ‚ÄúThat's right, Mr. Chen. Dark Souls is a big production with top-tier graphics, plot, and action. But Don't Starve is clearly a small-scale production and lacks that external appeal to make players keep playing. Won't it turn players away right from the start?‚Äù

‚ÄúBy the way, why do you all think this is a game that makes you suffer?‚Äù Chen Xu was a little speechless.

The slightly eerie art style, coupled with it being a survival game.

What with mental breakdowns, freezing to death from cold, starving to death from hunger, and wild monster attacks,

One could say that from Chen Xu's introduction, it felt like the protagonist was constantly facing the threat of death.

Considering Dark Souls, Silent Hill PT, and the tear-jerking The one with the big hammer that Chen Xu developed previously, along with the Death Mode recently launched.

Subconsciously, they immediately thought of suffering.

‚ÄúDifficulty will definitely be present, but unlike Dark Souls (informal), players will have choices.‚Äù Chen Xu explained, a little speechless.

Did he look like the kind of game designer who enjoyed tormenting players? It was too much; he'd lost all their trust!

‚ÄúAlright, let me give you a detailed explanation of this game's design direction. The game's core revolves around survival, encouraging players to explore, collect...‚Äù

After explaining the design philosophy of Don't Starve to everyone, they all went back to their respective work.

In his previous life, Don't Starve could be said to be a very unique small-scale game production.

Although it was a small-scale game production, its sales volume was even more astonishing than some AAA-tier games.

In his previous life, on the Steam Platform, Don't Starve's sales volume exceeded ten million.

Later, after being distributed by Tencent and launched on the WeGame Store, its sales volume surpassed one million in the first month, and then quickly exceeded three million in a very short time.

Of course, there were reasons for this booming success. Initially, Don't Starve was only a single-player game. Although it possessed a unique charm, it lacked a breakout point.

It wasn't until the release of the Online Multiplayer Version that Don't Starve's sales volume truly experienced explosive growth.

Choosing such a game as the team's next development project.

On one hand, it was due to Don't Starve's excellence and charm, and on the other, it was for the future strategy of the Nebula Games Store.

Using Don't Starve as a touchstone to gauge players' enthusiasm for survival and sandbox games.

As for sandbox games, there were actually many other choices, such as the hugely popular Valheim from his previous life, and of course, Minecraft and Terraria.

But considering that sandbox mode hadn't yet become popular in the Parallel World, although Chen Xu had already noticed some games with sandbox attributes appearing.

Yet, they hadn't formed an independent genre.

Players were still relatively unfamiliar with this game mode.

Launching Don't Starve first was considered a touchstone.

Because compared to the games mentioned earlier, Don't Starve had one most core characteristic.

Namely, its objective was very clear.

As a sandbox game with a high degree of freedom, new players entering the game who hadn't played similar titles would very likely be directly bewildered and dumbfounded.

In his previous life, many Minecraft players entered the game and at first couldn't even understand the difference between Creative (mode) and Survival Mode.

Not to mention all sorts of complex tools; many people couldn't even make a crafting table.

For most players just entering the game, they basically only knew two things: digging dirt in place and chopping down trees.

Although it was partly related to the game tutorial, on the other hand, it was also due to excessive freedom, which left players who were still immature in sandbox gameplay feeling bewildered.

Don't Starve, on the other hand, was more direct, whether it was survival, adventure, or cooperation.

It immediately told players the game's core: to explore and gather resources as much as possible in an unknown world, build various means of production, and strive to survive for the longest time.

This directly gave players a Main Objective: survival.

Centered around this Main Objective, players wouldn't feel completely bewildered, because they would know that their purpose in the game was survival, and what they needed to do was achieve this objective.

And through this objective, players would gradually experience the Gameplay of a sandbox game and discover the rich content within Don't Starve.

There were various scene maps, including plains, swamps, grasslands, mining areas, forests, caves, and so on. Different terrain would yield different production elements, and the monsters generated on the terrain would also vary.

In addition, the game's basic rules also included Season Settings.

In spring, it would rain frequently, and crops would grow faster than in other seasons.

Summer, combustibles would undergo spontaneous combustion, and the character's body temperature would rapidly rise, leading to a Heatstroke State.

Winter, crops would grow slowly, the character's body temperature would rapidly drop, leading to a Frostbite State.

Coupled with certain Random Map Generation Settings, every player's survival journey would be a different experience.

On the other hand, as a sandbox game, another part of the fun came from various MODs.

In his previous life, Minecraft was able to remain long-lasting for over a decade, and various MODs could be said to have played a crucial role.

In this area, the Nebula Games Platform's Workshop (modding platform) had already laid the foundation through Overcooked (Chinese title) and Fall Guys.

Currently, Overcooked (Chinese title) and Fall Guys still maintained considerable popularity among players, primarily due to the creation of their MOD community.

Platform users and the Workshop (modding platform) were all in place.

All that remained was to see the Player Performance Feedback that Don't Starve would bring.

(End of chapter)
