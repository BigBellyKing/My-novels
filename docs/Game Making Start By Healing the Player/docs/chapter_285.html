Relevant terms had mostly been agreed upon. And it wasn't just Shark TV; several other domestic live streaming platforms had directly collaborated to host it. The name of the tournament was the Golden Monthly Tournament. Held once a month, the prize money would be provided by Shark TV and other major platforms, along with some equipment sponsors.

The tournament was divided into 4v4 team matches and solo matches. Of course, the 4v4 team matches weren't a free-for-all brawl, but rather arena matches. As for the solo tournament, it would span four weeks: the first week for preliminary rounds, the second and third weeks for the group stage, and then players would be eliminated or placed into the winner's and loser's brackets based on their points. The group stage would use a Best of 2 format, with 3 points for a win, 1 for a draw, and 0 for a loss. The elimination round would be Best of 5, and the semi-finals and finals would be Best of 7.

The first monthly tournament adopted an open selection system. In the future, it would use a combined invitation and open selection model. Any Warcraft player with a Ladder Score of 2000 or higher could sign up.

This wasn't entirely surprising; after all, ordinary players and high-level players still had a significant gap compared to those who treated it as their profession. For most players, entertainment was a hobby, but for competitors, it was a career. The participating players were mostly familiar faces from other RTS games who had transitioned into Warcraft. In this aspect, Chen Xu had to admit that the Parallel World was indeed much more professional in organizing competitive tournaments. Chen Xu believed that the reason the RTS genre maintained such a strong presence in the Parallel World was closely related to its mature esports ecosystem.
