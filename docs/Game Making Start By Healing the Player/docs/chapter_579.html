In reality, it was completely useless in a fight; while it appeared formidable and its lethality was high enough that one hit could practically take out half your health. But its construction, half machine in the upper body and flesh in the lower, made its movements extremely inconvenient; its turning speed was even slower than when facing the noblewomen in the ancient castle. Moreover, its attack methods were just its 'three standard moves': a straight charge combined with a spinning flame breath. After a little familiarization and using the terrain to control Ethan to circle around it, it was basically nothing to worry about.

Compared to the Fan Monster, what concerned Song Ren more was Heisenberg's dialogue during this time, and the diary found later in the basement. Evidently, no matter the game world, both protagonists and NPCs shared a good habit of keeping a diary.

Miranda, for some unknown reason, learned about Rose, so in order to abduct Rose, Miranda transformed into Chris, forcefully entered the house, much like the event where Chris shot Mia, thus triggering this series of events.

Using his own methods, he intended to seize Rose and turn her into a weapon to definitively kill Miranda.
