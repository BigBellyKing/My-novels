Facing the Great Jagras before him, Zhang Yi was starting to lose a bit of patience.

This boss acted just like the bosses in Dark Souls' Death Mode, perfectly embodying the concept of 'If the enemy doesn't move, I don't move'.

Pulling out a Whetstone from his item pouch, Zhang Yi sharpened his Great Sword. During his earlier pursuit, he'd been harassed by a few Crown-crested Dragons, which had reduced his sword's sharpness.

Shouldering his Great Sword, Zhang Yi charged straight at the Great Jagras.

Looking at the numerical design of his health bar dropping, Zhang Yi felt a bit surprised.

The collision from the Great Jagras had shaved off roughly one-tenth of his health bar.

Clearly, this boss posed a real threat.

Still, despite that, Zhang Yi felt no worry at all.

His margin for error was still very generous.

Unlike Dark Souls, where you'd be nearly dead after two hits and finished after three, he could easily withstand seven or eight hits like this!

And most importantly, unlike Dark Souls, which confined battles to specific zones, Monster Hunter: World allowed free movement during combat without area restrictions.

When the Great Jagras attacked, during the charging animation, his character entered a hyper armor state, allowing him to tank the hit and trade damage.

"True! Charged Slash!" Zhang Yi had already assumed his stance, ready to strike.

On-screen, his Hunter character raised the massive Great Sword with both hands, building momentum.

Clang! The Level 3 Charged Slash indicator activated in the game.

In an instant, the Great Sword's most powerful True Charged Slash came crashing down!

In Zhang Yi's mind, he already pictured the Great Jagras being one-shot by this devastating blow, instantly crippled and defeated.

But in the next moment, the Great Jagras suddenly shifted its Aggro, twisted its rear, and lunged toward the nearby Palico.

At the same time, his Hunter character, still wielding the oversized Great Sword, executed a spinning slash.

Yet, despite this, Zhang Yi couldn't really blame the Palico.

Since he hadn't turned off damage indicators, he noticed the Palico was actually dealing quite a decent amount of damage.

Not only were its movements sluggish, but its damage output wasn't as high as expected.
