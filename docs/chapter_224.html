Chapter 324: For the Players, I Can Endure It (Please subscribe for monthly passes)

"Damn it! That's absolutely insane!"

Along with the video's final fade-out, Song Ren's voice, full of shock, came through the voice channel.

"This is called Modern Warfare? This is a World War, isn't it? It's too crazy!" Zhang Yi responded at this moment.

At the same time, he couldn't help but click replay.

This short promotional video, less than two minutes long, brought him an overwhelming sense of shock.

This video was not only played domestically but also overseas.

After all, as Huanyu VR devices' flagship title, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare wasn't just competing for the domestic market, but for the global market.

This time, the game publisher for overseas markets was still Target Software.

However, something was a little different from before.

That is, Target Software now belonged to Nebula Games.

Simply put, Nebula Games acquired a portion of Target Software's shares and is now the largest shareholder.

If it came to selling, Target Software certainly wouldn't want to sell, as their profits were steadily increasing.

But the key point was that the majority of Target Software's overseas revenue came from Nebula Games' titles.

And now that Nebula Games had proposed this plan, it was certainly fine if they didn't agree, but future cooperation would be difficult.

So Target Software, after some consideration, thought, "Why don't we just sell it?"

It's just becoming Nebula Games' shape, and once they adapt to it, it might even be more comfortable.

Under aggressive promotion, this promotional trailer also landed on major overseas video websites, accompanied by multi-language subtitles.

Overseas players were also incredibly excited to watch it.

After all, Nebula Games was no longer an unknown entity.

Earlier titles like Dark Souls, followed by Minecraft, Monster Hunter: World, Warcraft, and the recent Rainbow Six Siege, all received significant attention overseas.

At the same time, among the three most popular game genres overseas: gun, car, and ball,

Call of Duty: Modern Warfare belonged to the most widespread genre: gun.

These explosive scenes instantly ignited the passion of countless overseas players.

Especially those incredibly wild scenes, which sparked global discussion.

Domestic players:

"My god! Is this Chen Xu's new game? It's absolutely insane!"

"I can only say I'll buy it like crazy! And it feels like a completely different style from Rainbow Six Siege!"

"The White House falling, the Eiffel Tower collapsing, the Space Station disintegrating, cities nuked... is there anything Chen Xu doesn't dare to do!?"

"Yes! Chen Xu wouldn't dare to set the background in China! Forget bombing famous buildings, Chen Xu wouldn't even dare to damage flowers and grass!"

"Ahem, be a bit more farsighted! It's not that Mr. Chen doesn't dare, but it's a requirement for the bigger picture. After all, Huanyu VR is aiming for the global market, and setting the background overseas is primarily to better capture the overseas market."

"Never mind all that, I'm more curious about the gameplay content of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare. Is it more focused on multiplayer or single-player?"

"Multiplayer or single-player, I don't know, I just want it to be exciting. Rainbow Six Siege is fun, but I really can't play it."

"Can't play it +1. I'm more useful watching cameras after dying than when I'm alive."

There were also many discussions overseas.

"Atari just revealed the promotional video for Storm Action, and Nebula Games released content for Call of Duty: Modern Warfare right after, it's so cool!"

"Haha, you're right about the rivalry between Zeus and Huanyu. I was originally going to buy Zeus, but now I have to say I'll have to wait and see. The Call of Duty: Modern Warfare trailer is just too cool!"

"But this company's trailers, they won't actually be misleading, will they? I've been fooled many times before."

"My friend, do you know Dark Souls, Warcraft, Minecraft, and Rainbow Six Siege? These games are all made by Nebula Games, and they never falsify anything in their promotional videos."

"Yes, I specifically went to a Chinese website to learn more about this game company, especially their BOSS, who is also the lead designer for these games. In China, he has a nickname 'Warrior of Love,' meaning that every game contains his love for the players, so he would never do anything to make players uncomfortable."

"My goodness, that's so cool! But even so, to be honest, I'll still have to wait for the game to come out to draw conclusions. However, I have to say that I'm even more excited for Call of Duty: Modern Warfare now."

Clearly, the quality of this game's CG was absolutely world-class, showing many players what "ten years of game development, nine years of CG" truly means.

But while it was cool, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare didn't show much in terms of gameplay, and many players didn't understand what kind of game it truly was.

This was also within Chen Xu's expectations; this trailer was primarily to stir up players' enthusiasm.

To let players know what kind of explosive content this game, named Modern Warfare, would contain.

..............

News about Call of Duty: Modern Warfare spread worldwide.

Players engaged in various discussions.

At this time, Chen Xu was in the Nebula Games office, working with his team on the plot arrangement.

For the game's plot, Chen Xu adopted two presentation modes.

The first was pre-rendered CG cutscenes.

For example, when a scene change required pre-loading, CG cutscenes would be used to help players understand the story's plot.

The second was real-time story progression.

That is, players in game levels would communicate with NPC characters around them to reveal the game's plot.

Rather than simply clearing a level and then showing a real-time rendered animation.

Doing this had both advantages and disadvantages.

The advantage was a smoother player experience.

The disadvantage was that if a player's attention wandered, for example, if they scratched their foot mid-game, and an NPC character happened to be speaking,

Then some content might be missed.

There was no good solution for this, as everything has pros and cons.

A trade-off had to be made between the two.

Chen Xu's choice was to make the player's game experience smoother.

Furthermore, in VR games themselves, player concentration is higher than in PC platform games.

To some extent, this compensated for the disadvantage.

What remained was the story aspect.

"Mr. Chen, if this plot is written, players will probably send you razor blades again!"

Yang Xin, responsible for enriching the story in the office, couldn't help but exclaim as she looked at the story outline summary.

Because it was simply too brutal.

Just the highlighted points and script content already made her, being somewhat emotional, uncomfortable.

And as a VR game, with its high sense of immersion.

Players' emotions would definitely explode!

"There's no other way. All of this is to bring players a better gaming experience. For the players, I can endure these misunderstandings," Chen Xu said with a smile.

Sending razor blades?

Express delivery companies wouldn't even send them!

Moreover, Nebula Games' security forces had been strengthened before the New Year.

(End of Chapter)
