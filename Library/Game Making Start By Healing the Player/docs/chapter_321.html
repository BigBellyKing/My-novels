After confirming some development details for Call of Duty: Modern Warfare with the team, Chen Xu returned to his office to refine some subsequent game content. In Chen Xu's view, the gameplay of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare was already quite mature. Moreover, compared to other FPS games currently on the market, many designs in Call of Duty: Modern Warfare were actually quite advanced. As for comparing it to Rainbow Six Siege, which had just launched, they were completely different types of games, and their player bases were also distinct. Firstly, the 'breathing to heal' mechanic was not originally pioneered by the Call of Duty series. The shield design in Halo was the embryonic form of 'breathing to heal,' but it was indeed popularized by Call of Duty. The greatest benefit of this setting was that it kept players' attention and focus on combat. After taking damage, depending on the severity of the injury, the screen would turn blood-red and black-and-white, but as long as they didn't die immediately, after a few seconds out of combat and not taking damage, they could recover health by taking deep breaths. This allowed players to immerse themselves more deeply in the game experience and also made the game's pace very fast. In typical FPS games, if a player was injured, the most likely action was to look for health packs. This would create a sense of discontinuity in the player's experience, as the tension and tight combat of the story and level design would be directly fragmented. But with 'breathing to heal,' players could remain fully engaged in combat throughout. Additionally, there were settings in multiplayer, such as various firearm modifications, including infrared, night vision, and other attachments. And Call of Duty's unique killstreak reward system, where players could use high-tech items after achieving killstreaks. Such as UAV drones that could detect other players' positions and essentially enable 'wallhacks,' targeted bombing via pagers, calling in supply drops from the air, RPGs, flamethrowers, and so on. In his previous life, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare became popular not only because most games at the time were still rooted in World War I and II settings, and the game's own story was excellent. The wide array of modern weapons was also a reason for its appeal to players. If the story propelled Modern Warfare to its peak, then the multiplayer mode was the capital that made it an annual Call of Duty title. High-intensity, fast-paced combat ‚Äì this was the core philosophy of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare. Of course, some specific maps that led to everyone camping, searching for ages only to be blown up by a landmine, were exceptions.
