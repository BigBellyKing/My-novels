Outlast had made Mount Massive Asylum a nightmare for many. As for the later Silent Hill PT, it was, after all, just an incomplete DEMO.

Now, faced with this game called Resident Evil, Song Ren was both curious and a little scared. After pacing back and forth a couple of times, Song Ren finally decided to test the waters. There was an old saying: 'Since we're already here.' He had specifically waited until midnight to play this, so shouldn't he go in and take a look?

Besides, unlike the Mount Massive Asylum back then, at least it was broad daylight here, and the surroundings were bright. Looking at the environment, Song Ren let out a slight sigh of relief, feeling relatively at ease here.

Song Ren controlled Ethan as he walked deeper into the forest path, encountering many mosquitoes and insects along the way. However, there were no high-tension scenes. Eventually, a large villa appeared in sight, but a big iron gate blocked the way. He went to investigate; the iron gate was locked with chains, making it impossible to open from the front.

‚ÄúIsn't this the same old trick from Outlast?‚Äù Song Ren muttered to himself, looking at the impassable main gate and the very obvious small path beside it. He was familiar with this!

Continuing forward, as he rounded a corner, Song Ren saw a figure flash by. But this time, it didn't scare him. Why? Because the figure was too fast; he only heard footsteps and by the time he turned his head, it was gone.

He followed the path further, and along the way, Song Ren also found some decayed corpses and various animal carcasses bound together with blades and iron chains. Under normal circumstances, Song Ren felt he wouldn't be able to handle it. He had such good luck! He actually found a treasure map fragment right at the beginning!

Song Ren expressed great surprise, which even washed away some of the lingering fear in his heart. Of course, this good mood lasted only until he opened the front door of the house. The outside scene was bright, but after opening the door to the house, Song Ren fell silent. Standing at the doorway, the room inside was pitch black, not at all welcoming!

And the most crucial point was that the visual presentation of Resident Evil was truly excellent, no less than the previous Uncharted. The moment he pushed open the door, he could clearly see dust particles floating in the air under the light. It was a recording from three years ago by an unknown production crew, with three people heading to a haunted house for a show. In the original game content, this segment allowed the player's perspective to become Clancy, the cameraman in the videotape. However, in the VR version, Chen Xu did not use this mode.

Mainly, this change in perspective would actually make players feel a sense of detachment. At the same time, the content of this segment was not very important; its main purpose was to introduce some background to the player, reveal some puzzle-solving gameplay, and indicate the path forward. So Chen Xu also had the player, like Ethan, sit in front of the TV and watch the videotape. And actually, in terms of horror, watching the videotape was even more intense than becoming one of the people in the tape.
