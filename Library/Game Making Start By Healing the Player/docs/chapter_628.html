During this period, World of Warcraft had also been continuously promoted.

For example, the map in the game: Azeroth.

Compared to the Azeroth map scroll that only flashed by in the first promotional video.

Thunder Bluff, Stormwind, Ironforge, Silvermoon City, Exodar...

These familiar place names, and some that had not been mentioned in previous Warcraft games, nor appeared in Nebula World, but were presented as collectible content, now appeared before players.

At the same time, within Nebula World, many players also discovered that more content had been unlocked.

For instance, some NPCs could now be interacted with, and from their mouths, players could uncover rumors about the continent of Azeroth.

With bits of information released, it's fair to say that many players were beside themselves with excitement.

Some players were so thrilled that they even chose to return to the PC platform to experience Warcraft once more.

Many years had passed, and while Warcraft's visual presentation was no longer top-tier.

Its rich RPG maps still managed to get many players a little addicted after playing for a while.

Indeed, thanks to the stimulating news of World of Warcraft, this six-year-old game once again became the game with the most concurrent online players under the Nebula Games Platform.

And just as players were debating when World of Warcraft would be released, and if Chen Xu was just making grand promises.

Players who once again logged into Nebula World in Huanyu VR immediately became excited.

Because new content had appeared.

What excited them even more was that, compared to previous promotional videos.

This time, the subject of the CGI animation was someone they were very familiar with.

That familiar man, whether liked or disliked, had awakened.

Six years had passed in reality, but only four years in the game world, and Arthas reappeared before players.

By this point, Arthas had successfully merged with the power of the Lich King.

This also announced his official farewell to his past.
