Chapter 117: You Do It Then!

Exited the game, created a new character.

Let the character '123' be a thing of the past, and the brand new character 'aaa' would become a new star, carrying the flame of hope.

This time, Zhang Yi didn't bother with detailed character customization. Because the Knight class inherently comes with a set of armor and a sword and shield, his head was directly covered by the helmet. This way, his masterful character customization wouldn't be visible anyway, so he simply skipped it. As for the burial gifts, the branch he chose before might have some special use, but he wasn't clear about it and would have to figure it out later. But one thing he was certain of was that it was absolutely useless against Gundyr the Ash. So this time, Zhang Yi and Wang Han thought about it and chose the Life Ring, which boosts HP. After all, when you don't know what other items do, choosing equipment that increases your health is always the right choice!

Skipping the opening cutscene, Wang Han, sitting in front of the computer, felt a sense of peace watching 'aaa' in the game, clad in armor and wielding a sword and shield. An inexplicable confidence surged within her! Along the way, just like before, she dealt with the Hollows one by one. Then she picked up the pulse for focus, lit the first bonfire, ran beside the jumping platform, jumped onto the coffin, and retrieved the white item, a wedge-shaped stone fragment of unknown use. As for the Crystal Lizard on the other path, Wang Han currently had no intention of bothering it. After all, every wrong has its perpetrator, and it wasn't that Crystal Lizard that tormented her. Her main target now was still Gundyr the Ash. Passing through the stone gate, she entered the courtyard.

In the newly restarted save file, Gundyr the Ash was still kneeling as before. The only change was the bloodstains in the courtyard, visible from all directions. Wang Han once again observed the death animations of other players. They were mostly similar, but one thing made Wang Han a little nervous: she noticed quite a few players wearing knight armor among them. Some other players had died horribly, because on one side of the courtyard were piled gravestones and candlelight, and on the other was a bottomless cliff. Through the bloodstain phantoms left by other players, Wang Han saw many players rolling their way to the cliff edge, only to be swept away by something. This made Wang Han decide to keep a little distance from the cliff during the battle. Being beaten to death by Gundyr the Ash wasn't shameful; after all, it wasn't the first time. But being killed by the environment? That would be truly embarrassing. After all, the arena wasn't some narrow path, but a rather large courtyard.

Taking a deep breath, Wang Han controlled 'aaa' to step forward and pull the Coiled Sword from Gundyr the Ash's chest. After drawing the Coiled Sword, Wang Han didn't immediately retreat to create distance.
