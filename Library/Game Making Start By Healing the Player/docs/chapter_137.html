At this moment, Chen Xu was clearly not actively preparing a sequel as players imagined. Instead, he was currently slacking off.

Regarding Dark Souls' random mode, after Chen Xu communicated with Qin Yi, it was primarily Qin Yi and his team who were responsible. Developing the new mode didn't take too long, basically less than a week.

The main workload was concentrated on testing, specifically the consequences of various BOSSes randomly appearing in different scenarios, and whether this would prevent players from continuing the game. If it were a MOD from the Workshop, it naturally wouldn't need to be so detailed. If progress got stuck, players could simply exit the game, abandon that save file, and start a new one. However, this mode was officially launched by Nebula Games, which meant it was part of the game's official content. Therefore, if situations arose where players couldn't play smoothly, that would clearly be Nebula Games' responsibility.

So, this part had to be thoroughly tested before going online.

As for the new mode, players could customize it. For example, they could choose to only randomize item drops, meaning the game's progression would remain the same as before. Monsters and BOSSes would be identical, with no changes except for the items dropped after being defeated.

Players could also choose to only randomize enemy spawns, excluding some specific BOSSes, such as the Soul of Cinder at the Kiln of the First Flame and High Lord Wolnir in the Catacombs. All other enemies and BOSSes in the game would appear randomly. It's possible that when you crawl out of the Cemetery of Ash, you might not face a group of Hollows, but instead a group of Black Knights, Outrider Knights, and Lothric Knights.

Of course, both options could be enabled for a thoroughly exciting game. Players could then test whether they were playing "European Soul" or "African Soul."

......................

Without much fanfare, as if it were just a routine small update, Chen Xu announced the launch of Dark Souls' new mode on his official Weibo account.

As for the overseas version, it had already been handed over to Target Software. After passing reviews in various countries, it could also go online. Factoring in time differences, the latest it would be available was an interval of about three to four days.

Below Chen Xu's and Nebula Games' official Weibo accounts, the text content of this update was released. Initially, players didn't pay much attention, thinking it was just simple adjustments to some numerical values and multiplayer modes. But after seeing the update text, many players were a bit taken aback.
