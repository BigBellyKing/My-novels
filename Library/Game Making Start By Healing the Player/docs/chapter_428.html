Chen Xu wasn't clear what kind of technology this mode was, or how it was implemented. However, he understood the gist of it. Just as he had said before, the biggest advantage of VR gaming pods was the immersive experience.

But when it came to certain special games, sometimes excessive immersion wasn't ideal. The most direct example was horror games. Dying purely from fright was almost nonexistent; it was more likely to trigger other conditions, such as heart disease. Furthermore, few players actively sought the thrill of extreme fright. But not seeking it didn't mean it wouldn't happen. For instance, in the 'Warzone' mode of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare, which Chen Xu previously developed, while players enjoyed the parachuting gameplay, it wasn't ideal for those with acrophobia. That's why an automatic parachuting function was specifically added for it. This also included the Dark Souls port to the VR platform, where some grim scenes like the Undead Asylum were optimized to ensure players wouldn't be too scared. This new technology, however, was designed to ensure players had a better experience in regular games, preventing overly intense emotions due to excessive immersion that could lead to physical and mental strain.
